Traber was selected to the Baltimore Orioles in the 21st round (544th overall) of the 1982 Major League Baseball draft. Traber played in four seasons with Baltimore Orioles in 1984-89. During this time, the nickname of his was “The Whammer”. The most sluggish time of his career with Baltimore, Traber found success through the switch to a V neck jersey. Then he was enjoying a best-ever run of form that saw him smash out 17 runs and 6 homers that were batted into (RBI) and hit .340 in his first 13 games following being called up at the end of the season of. Traber’s performance in Baltimore was hampered by a faulty timing, as the experienced first baseman Eddie Murray, wasn’t ready to retire, and Traber was eager to get a chance to play at that position. Moreover, he did not receive any training for being an outfielder at Minor League Baseball (MiLB) according to the manager of his team Earl Weaver lamented. Therefore Traber’s options were restricted to being a reserve first baseman, DH, or pinch-hitter.

Following Traber’s MLB time, Traber took part in baseball games in Japan with his team the Kintetsu Buffaloes in 1990 and 1991, as well as one season with Monterrey from the Mexican League in 1993. The time he spent in Japan is most likely to be remembered because of an incident where Traber was seen rushing the mound after to being struck by a ball, and then chased the pitcher that was retreating towards the outfield of the Akita Yabase Baseball Stadium. After chasing the pitcher again and being thrown off of his balance when running, and was struck into the face on the way down by director, Masaichi Kaneda. This footage is available over the Internet.

Traber’s involvement with sports talk radio began after his retirement from professional sports. He served as a television color analyst for Fox Sports’ regional coverage of the Arizona Diamondbacks during the team’s World Series victory season of 2001, for which he was awarded a World Series ring. Traber was also part-time color analyst for the 2001 World Series radio broadcast. He can be heard cheering in celebration behind Greg Schulte during the famous play-by-play call of “A little blooper… Base hit! Diamondbacks Win!” for Luis Gonzalez’s game-winning hit. Traber continued announcing in 2002 and 2003. Currently, Traber hosts “The Afternoon Sports Beat” and “Total Dominance Hour,” shows on WWLS-FM radio station in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma. He sometimes refers to callers as Yardbirds.
