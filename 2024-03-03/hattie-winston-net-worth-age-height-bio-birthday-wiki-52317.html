Top Facts about Hattie Winston

  • Hattie Mae Winston was born on March 3, 1945 in Lexington, Mississippi.
  • She majored in music at Howard University.
  • She was an original member of the Negro Ensemble Company and acted on Broadway in a number of productions, including The Me Nobody Knows, The Two Gentlemen of Verona and I Love My Wife.
  • Winston served as a cast member of the PBS children’s series The Electric Company from 1973 to 1977.
  • She won the role of housekeeper Gloria Davis in TV series Homefront, appearing from 1991 to 1993.
  • Winston provided the voice of Lucy Carmichael in Nickelodeon’s Rugrats and its spin-off series All Grown Up!.
  • She played Margaret Turk, the mother of Dr. Christopher Turk, on the sitcom Scrubs from 2000 to 2010.
  • Winston portrayed Nurse Margaret Wybourn on the CBS sitcom Becker from 1998 to 2004.
  • She had roles in films such as Beverly Hills Cop III, Jackie Brown, True Crime and Living Out Loud.
  • Winston is married to Harold Wheeler, who served as musical director for Dancing with the Stars.
  • They have one daughter, Samantha.
