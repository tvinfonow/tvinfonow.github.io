Leon Wood is one of the most popular and richest Basketball Player who was born on March 25, 1962 in Colombia, United States. For the course of his NBA professional career Wood was in the league for 274 times and netted 1,742 points (6.4 points per game). Wood also played a brief European professional league experience with MTV 1846 Giessen in Germany with MTV 1846 Giessen, where Wood was signed to playoffs. He played in three games with a score in 77 point. He also was a player with CAI Zaragoza (ACB League) during the 1988-1989 season. Wood played for four seasons in the Continental Basketball Association, averaging 18.1 points and 8.5 assists over 118 games. He was selected second team All-CBA in the year 1990.

He was made an NBA official in 1996 . He is currently in his 22nd year as a referee for 2017-2018.

A 6’3″ point guard from Saint Monica Catholic High School, Wood was an All-American at California State University-Fullerton. He was selected by the Philadelphia 76ers as the 10th pick in the first round of the 1984 NBA draft and played in six NBA seasons for six different teams: the 76ers, Washington Bullets, New Jersey Nets, San Antonio Spurs, Atlanta Hawks and Sacramento Kings.
