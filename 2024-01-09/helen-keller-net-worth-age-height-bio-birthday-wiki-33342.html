How did Helen Keller lose her voice?

Helen Keller, who was born in 1880, lost her vision and her hearing when she was 19 months old, from an infection that was probably scarlet fever or meningitis. Like many toddlers at that age, she had some spoken language, which was presumably lost in the trauma of her illness.

Was Helen Keller Full deaf and blind?

At the age of 19 months, Keller became very ill with a high fever, leaving her totally deaf and blind.

Why Helen Keller is famous?

In addition to being a member of the Socialist Party, Keller was a founder of the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU). She was an outspoken advocate for women’s rights and a strong supporter of birth control, all radical views for a woman in the early 20th century.
