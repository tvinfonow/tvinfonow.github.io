Palmer Luckey Biography

Palmer Luckey is one of the most popular and richest Inventor who was born on September 19, 1992 in Long Beach, California, United States. In an effort to understand more about the technology of virtual reality, Luckey built an extensive private collection of over fifty various head-mounted displays. The majority of them were constructed in the early 1990s. When he was 16 He began to build VR headsets made by himself. To fund these projects he made at least $36,000 by repairing and reselling damaged iPhones, and also working as a groundskeeper, a youth sailing coach also a technician for computer repairs.

When he was a kid, he was taught at home by his mom, and had sailing lessons and showed a keen interest in engineering and electronics. He took college-level courses in Golden West College and Long Beach City College starting at around 14-15 years old and began attending classes in California State University, Long Beach in the year 2010. He was a writer and Online Editor of the student-run publication, Daily 49er.

During the Kickstarter campaign, Luckey demonstrated the Rift to gamers and the press at many gaming conventions, including PAX, Gamescom, and QuakeCon 2012.

John Carmack of id Software, a notable game developer famous for his work on the Doom and Quake videogame series, requested a prototype headset from Luckey, who lent it to Carmack free of charge. Carmack used it to demonstrate id Software’s Doom 3: BFG Edition on the device at the Electronic Entertainment Expo 2012. With the resulting attention of thousands of people suddenly drawn to the Rift, Luckey dropped out of university to focus on it full-time.

He established his own organization, the ModRetro Forums with a friend in 2009, establishing an online community dedicated to “portabilization”, a hobby that focuses on turning older hardware devices like gaming consoles and computers into portable devices that mix modern and traditional technology.

Palmer Freeman Luckey (born September 19th 1992) is an American businessman who is best known as the creator of Oculus VR and the designer for the Oculus Rift headset, a virtual reality head-mounted display widely acknowledged as the catalyst for the revival of the field of virtual reality. In the year 2017, Luckey was fired from Oculus and created the defense company Anduril Industries, a defense technology firm that focuses on autonomous drones as well as sensors for applications in the military. Luckey is 22nd on Forbes 2016’s list of America’s most successful entrepreneurs under 40.

Luckey was unhappy by the lack of quality in existing head-mounted displays on the market, that were characterized by low contrast and high latency, a small field-of-view, expensive and a huge weight and bulk. As a result, he began making new designs of his in the year 2009. He built the first prototype, known as PR1 aged 17 in his parent’s garage in 2010. The prototype had a 90-degree view perspective, low latency and haptic feedback built-in.

The Kickstarter campaign was successful, raising US$2.4 million, or 974% of its original target. After raising more than one million dollars, Brendan Iribe was hired by Luckey in August of 2012 to be CEO of Oculus. As a result of their successful campaign Oculus VR expanded, taking on more employees and a larger office space, but Luckey described his day-to-day process as not having “changed all that much,” remaining a “slow plod towards making this thing a reality.” Luckey continued to work on all aspects of the business, saying, “I have my hands in everything, from product engineering to game development to marketing,” Later, he shifted his focus towards virtual reality input hardware, calling it his “pet project”.
