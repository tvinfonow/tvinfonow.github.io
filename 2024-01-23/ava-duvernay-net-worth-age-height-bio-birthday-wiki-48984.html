Ava DuVernay Biography

Ava DuVernay is one of the most popular and richest Director who was born on August 24, 1972 in Los Angeles, California, United States. Award-winning filmmaker who has won major critical acclaim as the director of such films as 2012’s Middle of Nowhere and 2014’s Selma. For her work on Selma, she became the first African-American female to receive a Golden Globe nomination for Best Director.

Her film Selma depicts the 1965 Selma-to-Montgomery voting rights marches led by Martin Luther King Jr.

DuVernay was born on August 24, 1972, in Long Beach, California. She was raised by her mother, Darlene (née Sexton), an educator, and her stepfather, Murray Maye. The surname of her biological father, Joseph Marcel DuVernay III, originates with Louisiana Creole ancestry. She grew up in Lynwood, California (near Compton). She has four brothers and sisters.
