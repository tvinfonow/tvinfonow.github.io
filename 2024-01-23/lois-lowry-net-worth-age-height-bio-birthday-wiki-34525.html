Top Facts about Lois Lowry

  • Lois Ann Lowry is an American writer of books for children and young adults such as The Giver Quartet, Number the Stars, and Rabble Starkey.
  • She has won two Newbery Medals, for Number the Stars in 1990 and The Giver in 1994, and the Golden Kite Award and the Hope S. Dean Memorial Award.
  • Lowry’s books have been challenged or even banned in some schools and libraries, particularly The Giver.
  • She was born on March 20, 1937 in Honolulu, Hawaii, and her father was an Army dentist whose work moved the family to many parts of the world when Lowry was young.
  • She began writing at 40 years old in 1977 and has explored several complex issues, including racism, terminal illness, murder, the Holocaust, and the questioning of authority.
  • She was nominated three times for the Hans Christian Andersen Award, a biennial international award recognizing creators of children’s books.
  • In 2007 she won the Margaret Edwards Award from the American Library Association for her contribution to young adult literature.
  • She has been awarded honorary degrees from six universities, including a Doctorate of Letters by Brown University in 2014.
  • She has given the May Hill Arbuthnot Lecture, titled “UNLEAVING: The Staying Power of Gold”.
  • The Giver has been near the top of the America Library Association’s list of banned and challenged books since its publication in 1993.
