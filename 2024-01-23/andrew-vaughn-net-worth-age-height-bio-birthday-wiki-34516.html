Andrew Vaughn Biography

Andrew Vaughn is one of the most popular and richest Baseball Player who was born on April 3, 1998 in Santa Rosa, California, United States.

He enrolled at the University of California, Berkeley to play college baseball for the California Golden Bears. As a freshman, Vaughn played first base and made ten appearances as a pitcher. He hit 12 home runs while batting .349/.414/.555 and won the Pac-12 Conference Freshman of the Year Award. He played collegiate summer baseball in 2017 for the Victoria HarbourCats of the West Coast League. As a sophomore in 2018, Vaughn hit 23 home runs batting .402/.531/.819. Perfect Game/Rawlings named him their National Player of the Year, and he won the Pac-12 Conference Baseball Player of the Year Award and Golden Spikes Award. In 2019, his junior year, he hit .374/.539/.704 with 15 home runs and 50 RBIs in 52 games. After the 2018 season, he played collegiate summer baseball with the Wareham Gatemen of the Cape Cod Baseball League.

He played for the United States national baseball team in the 2019 WBSC Premier 12 tournament in November 2019.
