Mark Watson Biography

Mark Watson is one of the most popular and richest Comedian who was born on February 13, 1980 in England, England, United Kingdom. Satirical stand-up comedian and author who starred in The Mad Bad Ad Show and Improvisation My Dear Mark Watson. He won the first ever Panel Prize at the if.comeddies in 2006.

He co-starred in The Mad Bad Ad Show with Micky Flanagan .

Watson was born in Bristol to a Welsh mother and English father. He has younger twin sisters called Emma and Lucy and a brother, Paul. He attended Henleaze Junior school and then Bristol Grammar School, an independent day school where he won the prize of ‘Gabbler of the Year’, before going to Queens’ College, Cambridge, where he studied English, graduating with first class honours. At university he was a member of the Footlights and contemporary of Stefan Golaszewski, Tim Key and Dan Stevens. He was part of the revue which was nominated for the Best Newcomer category in the Perrier Comedy Awards at the 2001 Edinburgh Festival Fringe and also co-directed a revue with Key.

Watson has written six novels: Bullet Points (2004), A Light-Hearted Look At Murder (2007), Eleven (2011), The Knot (2012), Hotel Alpha (2015), and The Place That Didn’t Exist (2016). In addition, he has written the non-fiction book Crap at the Environment (2008), following his own efforts to halve his carbon footprint over the course of one year. In 2015, he released a graphic novel, Dan and Sam, with illustrations by Oliver Harud.

He has a son and a daughter with wife Emily Watson-Howes, a fellow writer and performer.
