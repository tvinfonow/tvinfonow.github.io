The Scottish Daily Mail has described Ross as the “trusted advisor” of Prince of Wales (who happens to be Duke of Cornwall). The Guardian described the Duchy in 2011 as a “target-oriented investment portfolio” headed by Bertie Ross, a PS200,000-a year chief executive. Ross oversees the equivalent to 91 full-time employees. Ross was challenged by members of the House of Commons Public Accounts Committee in 2005. Gerry Steinberg stated that Ross said the Prince used the money to pay his staff and expenses. This statement made headlines. Ross denied any wrongdoing.

Ross was made a Commander in the Royal Victorian Order during the 2006 New Year Honours and promoted to Knight Commander during the 2012 New Year Honours.
