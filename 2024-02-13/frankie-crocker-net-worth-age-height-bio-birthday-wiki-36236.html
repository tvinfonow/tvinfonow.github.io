Top Facts about Frankie Crocker

  • Frankie Crocker was a legendary American disc jockey.
  • He was born on December 18, 1937 in Buffalo, New York.
  • Crocker became famous for his radio show “The Chief Rocker”.
  • He worked at several radio stations including WBLS and WWRL.
  • Crocker was known for introducing new music to his listeners.
  • He coined the phrase “urban contemporary” to describe his style of music.
  • In 1979, he hosted the first nationally syndicated urban countdown show.
  • Crocker was also a television personality and actor.
  • He died on October 21, 2000 due to complications from diabetes.
  • His legacy continues to influence modern-day DJs and broadcasters.
