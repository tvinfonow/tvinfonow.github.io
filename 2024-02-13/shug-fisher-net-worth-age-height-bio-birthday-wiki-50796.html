Top Facts about Shug Fisher

  • Shug Fisher was born on September 26, 1907 in Grady County, Oklahoma in Tabler.
  • Fisher was nicknamed “Shug” by his mother because he was a sweet baby.
  • Fisher joined the Sons of the Pioneers in 1943, replacing Pat Brady and Lloyd Perryman drafted into the service.
  • Fisher wrote a number of songs for the Pioneers, including “Out On The Open Range” and “Gooseberry Pie”.
  • Fisher and Merle Travis co-wrote the song “Cincinnati Lou” in 1946.
  • Fisher had a career as a character actor in Western B-movies and feature films, including Roy Rogers’ singing cowboys movies.
  • Fisher was the voice of Uncle Pecos, an animated mouse, in the 1953 Tom and Jerry short, Pecos Pest.
  • Fisher made regular appearances on ABC-TV’s country music variety show, Ozark Jubilee between 1955 and 1960.
  • Fisher made 22 appearances on Gunsmoke from 1962 to 1974.
  • Fisher played Shorty Kellums in 19 episodes of The Beverly Hillbillies from 1969 to 1970.
  • Fisher appeared repeatedly on other television shows, such as Bonanza, The Virginian, Daniel Boone, and The Dukes of Hazzard.
  • Fisher died on March 16, 1984 after a lingering illness in Woodland Hills, Los Angeles.
  • Fisher was buried at Forest Lawn Memorial Park.
  • Fisher was the youngest of four children born to a Scots-Irish father and part-Choctaw mother.
