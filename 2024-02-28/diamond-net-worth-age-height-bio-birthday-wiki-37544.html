What rock is diamond found in?

Diamond is only formed at high pressures. It is found in kimberlite, an ultrabasic volcanic rock formed very deep in the Earth’s crust. The extreme pressures needed to form diamonds are only reached at depths greater than 150km.

Is diamond a rock or stone?

A diamond is a precious stone (mineral) that is considered a clear and generally colorless crystalline form of pure carbon, with the same carbon composition as graphite, but with a different structure. It is the hardest of naturally occurring substances.
