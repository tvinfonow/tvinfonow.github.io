Gino Jennings Biography

Gino Jennings is one of the most popular and richest Pastor who was born on February 10, 1963 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, United States. Jennings was born eighth of eight siblings on February 10, 1963. Both his parents served as clergy ministers at the church of his uncle’s father’s in Philadelphia. His father, known by his name Ernest Jennings was a bishop and at thirteen, after delivering his first sermon based on Hebrews 11:6, his young Gino was appointed assistant pastor at the church in the year 1975. When he was a teenager, following having received the baptism of Holy Spirit in the Holy Ghost’s name, he started preaching regularly, under the direction of his great-uncle and his father and began to preach in crusades and revivals at various churches in Philadelphia. Philadelphia along with his parents.

In the years 1986 to 1988 numerous members of his uncle’s church began to slander First Church and taunt Jennings and accuse him of being a fake teacher and claiming that he put too much focus on the doctrine of legalism. A number of church and ministry auxiliary organizations were established around the period, which allowed Jennings to lease auditoriums at public facilities in Philadelphia to hold First Church worship services and gatherings.

By March 1990, Jennings and First Church had also set up their own local radio broadcasting equipment within their church and began broadcasting audio tapes and live recordings of their services on local Philadelphia radio stations. First Church was also given a thirty-minute international broadcast on WWCR Nashville, and a one-hour broadcast on WTMR Camden, New Jersey. Jennings then named the broadcasts, “The Truth of God” claiming that that was the name God gave him to give as a title to the radio broadcast, to help them reach souls and inspire interest in any Christians willing to tune in and listen. By 1991, over 11 radio stations across several states and even international radio stations in other countries in the Caribbean, Africa, and Europe were broadcasting Jennings’ sermons to international listeners.

On April 12, 1991, Jennings’ father, Bishop Ernest Jennings died. The First Church Headquarters in Philadelphia hosted his funeral, and hundreds of people from all around the country came to pay their respects. Gino preached a very charismatic eulogy for his father which was heard on the radio and television across the country through the Truth of God broadcast.

He founded with the First Church of Our Lord Jesus Christ. (FCOOLJC) the 21st of May, 1984, in the basement in his family’s home. It started with just 15 members (family was not made up of). Many of his teenager and young adult acquaintances from college and high school and their families were a part of the congregation in 1984, too. In the years 1984 to 1988 a number members from the church of his great-uncle began to join and the church steadily increased. With time, several rooms in the basement as well as different areas of the home had been converted into overflow rooms.
