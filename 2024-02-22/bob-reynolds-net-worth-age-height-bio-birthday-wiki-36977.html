Bob Reynolds Biography

Bob Reynolds is one of the most popular and richest Saxophonist who was born on September 29, 1977 in Morristown, New Jersey, United States. Jazz tenor saxophonist and Grammy Award-winning member of the band Snarky Puppy. He’s known for critically-acclaimed albums like Can’t Wait For Perfect and A Live Life. He’s also a multiple-time ASCAP Young Jazz Composer Award winner; he’s worked with notable musicians like Aaron Goldberg and John Mayer.

He studied with fellow saxophone star George Garzone at the Berklee College of Music.

His 2006 album Can’t Wait for Perfect was voted Best Debut in the Village Voice jazz poll. Reynolds received a Grammy Award with Snarky Puppy in 2017, four ASCAP Young Jazz Composer awards, and Berklee’s Billboard Magazine Endowed Scholarship.
