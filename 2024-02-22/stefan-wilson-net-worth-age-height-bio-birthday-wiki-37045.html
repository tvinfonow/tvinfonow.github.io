Stefan James Wilson (born 20 September 1989 in Sheffield, UK) is a British racing driver from the UK. His younger brother is former Formula One and IndyCar Series driver Justin Wilson. He also was one of the winners from the 2007 McLaren Autosport BRDC Award for promising young British drivers.

After a lengthy karting experience when a kid, Stefan stepped up to open-wheel racing in the month of November and competed at the Formula Palmer Audi Autumn Trophy at Snetterton and Brands Hatch. Stefan slowly improved throughout his first six races, and ended the mini-series with a record ninth place. He also finished as the top rookie. He scored three top-six finishes , and also set the fastest time during the final race of the series at Brands Hatch.

Wilson signed to race a partial schedule in Firestone Indy Lights in 2009 for First Motorsports with cars prepared by Walker Racing that included six road and street courses. He qualified third at Long Beach but was sidelined by a mechanical problem towards the finish. His best finish was fourth in the wet/dry race at Toronto. In 2010 returned to the series to race for Bryan Herta Autosport. Wilson finished 11th in points despite missing one race due to funding issues and another due to a damaged racecar. He had a career-best finish of third in the season opener at St. Pete and ran the fastest race lap in Toronto.
