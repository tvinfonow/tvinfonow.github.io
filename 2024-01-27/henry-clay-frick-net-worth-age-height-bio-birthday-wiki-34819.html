Top Facts about Henry Clay Frick

  • Henry Clay Frick was an American industrialist and art collector.
  • He founded the H.C. Frick & Company coke manufacturing company.
  • Frick was a key figure in the Homestead Strike of 1892.
  • He built a grand mansion, known as Clayton, in Pittsburgh, PA.
  • Frick’s art collection included works by Rembrandt and Vermeer.
  • He donated his art collection to establish the Frick Collection museum in NYC.
  • The Frick Art Reference Library is also named after him.
  • Frick died at age 69 from a heart attack while on vacation in New York City.
  • His net worth at the time of his death was estimated at $225 million (equivalent to over $5 billion today).
  • The legacy of Henry Clay Frick continues through his philanthropic foundation, which supports education and cultural institutions around the world.
