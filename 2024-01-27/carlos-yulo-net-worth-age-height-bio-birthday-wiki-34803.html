Carlos Yulo Biography

Carlos Yulo is one of the most popular and richest Artistic Gymnast who was born on February 16, 2000 in Malate, Metro Manila, Philippines. Carlos Edriel Yulo was the son of Mark Andrew Yulo (Philippines) and Angelica Yulo (Malate). He was raised on Leveriza Street. He is the second child of five siblings. Drew, one of his younger brothers, is also a gymnast and tries to surpass his older brother’s achievements. Yulo was raised watching Filipino gymnasts compete at the Rizal Memorial Sports Complex, Malate.

He was allowed to leave his home in Japan by his parents and he went to Tokyo for training with Japanese coach Munehiro Kugaimiya. In 2017, he was in the spotlight when he competed in the 14th Junior Asian Championships, Bangkok, Thailand. He suffered a sprained ankle while training but he won gold in the parallel bars, after qualifying in fourth.

Yulo began competing as a senior in 2018, at 18 years old. In his Gymnastics World Cup debut in the 2018 series, he consistently performed well, winning a medal in the Melbourne, Baku, Doha, and Cottbus events. At the men’s floor exercise event at the 2018 Asian Games, he scored highest in the qualification phase but failed to secure a medal after finishing 7th in the final.

At the 2019 Southeast Asian Games, Yulo finished on the podium in every event winning gold in the all-around and floor exercise and silver in the pommel horse, still rings, vault, parallel bars, and horizontal bar.

In 2013, Yulo also entered Teikyo University, Itabashi, Tokyo to study literature. Yulo is now fluent in Japanese thanks to this academic endeavor. Yulo accepted a scholarship offer from the Japan Olympic Association in 2018 to train in Japan.

Carlos Yulo Net Worth

Carlos Yulo is one of the richest Artistic Gymnast from Philippines. According to our analysis, Wikipedia, Forbes & Business Insider, Carlos Yulo's net worth $5 Million. (Last Update: December 11, 2023)

Carlos Edriel Yulo, a Filipino artistic gymnast, was born February 16, 2000. He has won gold and bronze at the World Artistic Gymnastics Championships. With his 2018 floor exercise bronze medal, he is the first Filipino gymnast to win the World Artistic Gymnastics Championships. He also won the first gold medal for the Philippines on the same apparatus in 2019. He was also eligible for the 2020 Summer Olympics in Tokyo.

Yulo was seven years old when he joined the Gymnastics Association Philippines program. After his grandfather noticed him doing somersaults in the playground, Yulo was brought to the association. Yulo received free training at Rizal Memorial Complex as a child from the Philippine Sports Commission with Ricardo Ortero his coach. In 2008, he began competing. He competed in Palarong Pambansa as part of the National Capital Region’s Gymnastics Team. Although he did not win a medal at the first edition, he was able to reach the top podium in the second. Yulo won four gold medals at the Palarong Pambansa 2011, while another gold medal was earned for the Philippine National Games 2011.

At the 2018 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships in Doha, Yulo advanced to the all-around and floor exercise finals. He won bronze in the floor exercise becoming the first Filipino and the first male Southeast Asian gymnast to win a medal at the championships. At the 2019 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships in Stuttgart, Yulo claimed gold in the floor exercise finals. A historic feat as the first ever Filipino winning gold at a world gymnastics championship.
