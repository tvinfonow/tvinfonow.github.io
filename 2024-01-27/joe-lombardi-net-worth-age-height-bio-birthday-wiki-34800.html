Joe Lombardi (born November 7 1959) is an American basketball coach who is currently the head coach of the Indiana University of Pennsylvania (IUP) Crimson Hawks of Pennsylvania State Athletic Conference in NCAA Division II. Prior to assuming the head coaching job within IUP, Lombardi served as an assistant coach, which included his nine-year time with La Salle University and three seasons with Jamie Dixon at the University of Pittsburgh. For his 4th season as the Crimson Hawks in 2009-10, Lombardi took his team in the NCAA Men’s Division II Basketball Championship in which they were defeated against the Cal Poly Pomona Broncos. After the season, Lombardi was named Coach of the Year in 2010. In 2003, Lombardi joined the University of Pittsburgh staff under head coach Jamie Dixon. Lombardi was primarily focused on recruiting, scouting opponents, and player development. During his time at Pitt, the Panthers amassed a 76–22 record, played in the Big East Championship game in 2004 and 2005, and advanced to their third consecutive Sweet Sixteen in 2004. While with the Panthers, Lombardi was responsible for recruiting Gilbert Brown and Sam Young, who was a 2009 All-American and a 2009 second-round selection by the Memphis Grizzlies.

In April 2006, Lombardi was named the head coach at Indiana University of Pennsylvania. The Crimson Hawks compete in the Pennsylvania State Athletic Conference of NCAA Division II. Lombardi previously served as an assistant under Tom Beck for three seasons with IUP between 1984 and 1987.

After his time in IUP, Lombardi served as an assistant to Jim Baron at Saint Francis University in Loretto, Pennsylvania from 1987 until 1992. In his time in Saint Francis, the Red Flash experienced two of their greatest seasons in their 20-year history. In the 1990-91 campaign, they Red Flash recorded a 24-8 record, and won the Northeast Conference and earned the school’s first appearance at the NCAA’s Men’s Division I Basketball Championship as an #15 seed. At Saint Francis, Lombardi guided Mike Iuzzolino, who was chosen 35th overall by the Dallas Mavericks in the 1991 NBA draft.
