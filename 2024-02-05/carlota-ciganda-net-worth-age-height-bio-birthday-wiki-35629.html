Ciganda was an amateur player in several professional tournaments. She first participated in the 2005 Tenerife Ladies Open when she was 14. Although she didn’t make it to the final, she finished as the best Spanish participant in several professional tournaments. She finished eighth at the 2007 Open De Espana Femenino and third at the 2008 Tenerife Ladies Open, three shots behind Rebecca Hudson.

Ciganda had a great amateur career and won the British Ladies Amateur in 2007 Ciganda returned to the finals for 2009 but finished second to Azahara Munoz, her ASU teammate. Ciganda was the Spanish National age-group champion 2000-2006, and won the European Ladies Amateur Championships in 2004 and 2008. Ciganda was part of Spain’s 2006-2008 Espirito Santo Trophy team, where she finished second in the European Junior Solheim Cup teams (2008), 2005 and 2007, and fourth in the European Junior Ryder Cup teams (2006). As a freshman at Arizona State, she was part of the Sun Devils’ NCAA Championship team in 2009. She made conference history by becoming the first Pac-10 Champion in consecutive years in 2009 and 2010.

In her first full season on the 2012 Ladies European Tour, Ciganda won the ISPS Handa Order of Merit and was also named Players’ Player of the Year, as voted for by the LET members. She played in 19 tournaments, with two victories at the Deloitte Ladies Open and the China Suzhou Taihu Open and had ten additional top-10s finishes. Her efforts won her the LET Rookie of the Year award and she ended the season ranked second on the European Solheim Cup points list with total earnings of €251,289.95.
