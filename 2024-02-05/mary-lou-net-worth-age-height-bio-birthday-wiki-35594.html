Why is Mary Lou important?

Mary Lou took part in every era of jazz development, from the 1920s through the 1970s. She was an expert stride and boogie woogie pianist. She helped develop the Kansas City swing sound of the 1930s when she joined Andy Kirk’s Clouds of Joy.

What made Mary Lou Williams stand out?

Mary Lou had a whip-smart ear, superb sense of time and drama–and great determination. Notably, she continued to evolve through the eras of jazz to follow: swing, bebop and free jazz.
