He graduated from the University of Miami and began his career in public radio working for a collaborative project between WLRN and the Miami Herald, from 2004 to 2010.

In 2010, Johnson relocated to San Francisco to work for KQED, where he served as morning newscaster until early 2016. He was also a substitute host of KQED’s Forum and taught courses in podcasting at the UC Berkeley Graduate School of Journalism.
