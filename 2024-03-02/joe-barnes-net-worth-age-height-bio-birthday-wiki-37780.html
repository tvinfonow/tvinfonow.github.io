Joe Barnes Biography

Joe Barnes is one of the most popular and richest American Football Player who was born on December 18, 1951 in Fort Worth, Texas, United States. Joe Barnes (born December 18 1951) was a star quarterback for the Canadian Football League.

He relocated to Canada in 1976 , and began a five-year run playing for the Montreal Alouettes. He started by taking over the quarterback duties of Sonny Wade, but mostly was in charge by 1978, where he threw 1177 yards. Then in 1979, he threw for 2456 yards. He was the winner of the Grey Cup in 1977 and was the quarterback during the 1977 and 1979 Cup loss in the Cup to Edmonton Eskimos. He was sacked as the start as the quarterback of the team in the year 1980 in the hands of Gerry Dattilio after six games after which he was transferred with the Saskatchewan Roughriders. In the following season, he also played quarterback for his team the Green Riders with John Hufnagel After the 1981 season, he moved to Toronto.

In 1984, he was named an all-star. In 1985, he was traded to the Calgary Stampeders but the team struggled for wins. After 11 games he was returned to Montreal in a trade, now with the Montreal Concordes, and played with the briefly renamed and reborn Alouettes in 1986.

Barnes was selected to the Chicago Bears of the NFL in 1974, in 13th place (316th overall.) He only played in two matches with Chicago in 1974, and completed two of nine passes and punting one time for 27 yards.

It was with the Toronto Argonauts that Barnes enjoyed his greatest success in the team’s run and shoot offence. At first, due to injury and the excellent play of Condredge Holloway, Barnes played sparingly in 1982. Starting in 1983, he worked in tandem with Holloway. For two seasons, the tandem combined for over 5,000 yards. In 1983 Holloway passed for 3184 yards and Barnes, 2274. The next season the numbers reversed, with Barnes passing for 3128 yards and Holloway, 2231. Barnes helped lead Toronto to a long-awaited Grey Cup victory in 1983. As was his style, he came off the bench in the second half and engineered the game-winning drive and TD, a pass to Cedric Minter, in the final minutes. He was named the Grey Cup Most Valuable Player for his heroics.
