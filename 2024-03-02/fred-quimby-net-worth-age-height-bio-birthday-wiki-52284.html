Top Facts about Fred Quimby

1. Frederick Clinton Quimby was an American animation producer and journalist.
2. He is best known for producing the Tom and Jerry cartoon series, for which he won 7 Academy Awards.
3. Quimby started his career as a journalist and moved to MGM in 1927.
4. The idea for Tom and Jerry came from William Hanna and Joseph Barbera, who presented their proposal to Quimby.
5. Tom and Jerry was initially met with resistance from Quimby, but following the success of Puss Gets the Boot, Quimby agreed to make Tom and Jerry an official cartoon of the MGM cartoon studio.
6. Quimby was never involved in the creative process of the Tom and Jerry episodes and had a difficult relationship with animators.
7. After the production of Good Will to Men in 1955, Quimby retired from MGM.
8. Hanna and Barbera took over as the production title for the Tom and Jerry shorts.
9. MGM closed its cartoon division in 1957, and Gene Deitch and Chuck Jones produced more Tom and Jerry shorts in the 1960s.
10. Quimby died in Santa Monica, California, in 1965 and was buried at Forest Lawn Memorial Park in Glendale, California.
