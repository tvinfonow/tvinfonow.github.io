Top Facts about Robert Moses

  • Robert Moses was an influential urban planner in New York City.
  • He held multiple government positions throughout his career.
  • Moses is known for building numerous parks, highways, and bridges.
  • His projects displaced thousands of people, particularly low-income communities.
  • Moses’ vision prioritized automobiles over public transportation.
  • He faced criticism for his lack of consideration for community input.
  • Moses’ legacy includes the creation of Jones Beach State Park and Lincoln Center.
  • His proposed Lower Manhattan Expressway was met with significant opposition and ultimately failed to be built.
  • Moses’ influence declined in the 1960s as public opinion shifted towards community involvement in planning decisions.
  • Despite controversy surrounding his methods, many of Moses’ projects remain integral parts of New York City’s infrastructure today.

    What is Robert Moses known for?

    Robert Moses played a larger role in shaping the physical environment of New York City than probably any other figure in the 20th century. He constructed parks, highways, bridges, playgrounds, housing, tunnels, beaches, zoos, civic centers, exhibition halls, and the 1964-65 New York World’s Fair.

    How did Robert Moses destroy New York?

    Moses is blamed for having destroyed more than a score of neighborhoods by building 13 expressways across New York City and by building large urban renewal projects with little regard for the urban fabric or for human scale.

    What did Robert Moses destroy?

    Quite literally, Moses got rid of his critics by displacing more than 1,500 families to build the 7-mile expressway (Sedensky 2001). The massive trench created during construction (Figure 1) is the result of the destruction of Bronx homes.

    Why was Robert Moses so powerful?

    Moses’ staying power relied chiefly on two pillars: his popularity and the budgets he controlled. His popularity had for almost 30 years been unquestioned, and his twelve positions in city and state posts were largely outside anyone’s ability to fire him, even the Mayor and Governor.
