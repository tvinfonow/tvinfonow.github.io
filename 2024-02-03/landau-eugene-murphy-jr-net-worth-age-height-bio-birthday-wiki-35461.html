Landau Eugene Murphy Jr. Biography

Landau Eugene Murphy Jr. is one of the most popular and richest Jazz Singer who was born on August 11, 1974 in West Virginia, West Virginia, United States. Known best as the winner of the 2011 season of the NBC series America’s Got Talent, this jazz vocalist released his debut album, That’s Life, in late 2011. The recording peaked at #34 on United States music charts.

His performance of the Frank Sinatra hit “My Way” earned him the America’s Got Talent championship title and its accompanying one-million-dollar prize.

He worked as a car washer during the months leading up to his TV performances, and after his home had been robbed, he was nearly destitute when he arrived to audition for America’s Got Talent in 2010. Landau has five children Michael Cross, Marcus Murphy, Morgan Murphy, Terrick Murphy, and Logan Murphy. Landau has released three albums, and also obtained a Doctorate of Music. He continues to tour and sell out shows across the world.

After winning, Murphy signed to Sony Records and Columbia Records, and released his first album That’s Life on November 21, 2011. The album debuted at No. 34 on the Billboard 200, selling 28,000 copies, then fell to No. 41 the next week on the charts. It debuted at number one on the Billboard jazz charts, and also reached No. 1 in the Jazz category on Amazon.com. By May 2017, the album had sold 156,000 copies.

A native of Logan, West Virginia, he spent the later years of his childhood in Detroit, Michigan. When he returned to his hometown in his twenties, he married childhood friend Jennifer Carter.
