Jenny Berggren Biography

Jenny Berggren is one of the most popular and richest Opera Singer who was born on May 19, 1972 in Gothenburg, Sweden. Former lead singer of the group Ace of Base who recorded the 1990s hits “The Sign,” “All That She Wants,” and “Happy Nation.”

Almost as popular as her group were fellow Swedes A*Teens, comprised of singer Marie Serneholt and several others.

During the late 1980s, the Berggren siblings, along with Johnny Lindén and Nicklas Tränk, formed the techno band Tech Noir. The group eventually became Ace of Base. Berggren also attended university, with the intention of becoming a teacher. However, she still pursued a musical career, performing background vocals for symphonic rock band Masque while simultaneously preparing vocals to Ace of Base material, which had yet to be released. Berggren put her plans to become a teacher on hold when Ace of Base was signed to Danish label Mega Records, with the band’s debut album eventually selling more than 30 million copies worldwide.

On the night of 27 April 1994, Berggren and her mother were attacked in their home by a woman who had followed Berggren home. Berggren’s mother was stabbed in the hands, and the woman, a 21-year-old German named Manuela Behrendt, was banned from ever returning to Sweden.

She was born in Gothenburg, the third child of three children. Her parents are Göran Berggren, an X-ray technician, and his wife Birgitta Berggren.
