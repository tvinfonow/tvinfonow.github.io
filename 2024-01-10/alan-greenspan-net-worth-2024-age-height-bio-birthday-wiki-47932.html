Alan Greenspan Biography

Alan Greenspan is one of the most popular and richest Economist who was born on March 6, 1926 in New York City, New York State, United States. American economist who served as Chairman of the Federal Reserve of the United States from 1987 to 2006. His memoir, entitled, The Age of Turbulence: Adventures in a New World, was published in 2007.

He was first appointed Federal Reserve chairman by President Ronald Reagan in August 1987.

His parents were Herbert Greenspan and Rose Goldsmith. In 1997, he married Andrea Mitchell.

Why did Alan Greenspan lower interest rates?

The “Greenspan put” was a monetary policy strategy popular during the 1990s and 2000s under Greenspan. Throughout his reign, he attempted to help support the U.S. economy by actively using the federal funds rate to aggressively lower interest rates to fight the deflation of asset price bubbles.

Who was Alan Greenspan's first wife?
