Mary Harvey Biography

Mary Harvey is one of the most popular and richest Association Football Player who was born on June 4, 1965 in Palo Alto, California, United States. Mary Harvey (born June 4 1965) is an American retired soccer goalie. She was the goalie of choice for the 1991 U.S. Women’s National Team that won the first FIFA Women’s World Cup; as well as a player on that 1997 Olympic Gold Medal team. Harvey was able to retire from international competition in 1996 after her participation in the Atlanta Olympics.

The year 2008 was the time that Harvey quit FIFA to return to her home in the United States to become the Chief Operating Officer of Women’s Professional Soccer, the new professional women’s soccer team within the United States. Harvey was dismissed from WPS in the year 2010 after two seasons.

Outside of the field, Harvey was on U.S. Soccer’s Board of Directors for 12 years, with five times on its Executive Committee; and on the U.S. Olympic Committee. The year 2003 saw Harvey got a job with FIFA to be the Director of Development. She became one of the few women and the first American recruited by the international body that governs the sport to lead the division. In this capacity, Harvey was responsible for managing the US$160 million a year development budget of FIFA and other programs such including the Financial Assistance Program, the Goal Programme, and FIFA’s courses or education programs. She also was responsible for FIFA’s efforts to promote women’s football, futsal , and beach soccer, as well as to supervise FIFA’s sports medicine programs. Before joining FIFA, Harvey was the director of a US$15 million fitness and sports company and was employed in the field of management consulting working for Deloitte. and Accenture.
