Top Facts about Bob Gaudio

  • Robert John Gaudio was born November 17, 1942 in the Bronx, New York
  • He grew up in Bergenfield, New Jersey and was the keyboardist and backing vocalist of the pop/rock band The Four Seasons
  • He co-wrote The Four Seasons’ first No. 1 hit, “Sherry” in 1962
  • Gaudio wrote or co-wrote and produced the vast majority of The Four Seasons’ music
  • He was a member of the Royal Teens and met Frankie Valli and his group the Four Lovers while promoting the single “Short Shorts”
  • He and Valli formed The Four Seasons in 1960
  • Gaudio wrote/co-wrote and produced hits for other artists, including The Tremeloes, The Osmonds and Walker Brothers
  • He wrote and produced Frank Sinatra’s album Watertown in 1969
  • He wrote “Who Loves You” and “December 1963 (Oh, What a Night)” in 1975
  • Gaudio, Valli, Tommy DeVito and Nick Massi were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1990 and the Vocal Group Hall of Fame in 1999
  • He wrote and/or produced for Michael Jackson, Barry Manilow, Diana Ross and other artists
  • He wrote Peggy Sue Got Married and produced Jersey Boys, a musical play based on the lives of The Four Seasons
  • He was inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 1995
  • He received his high school diploma 50 years after dropping out in 2009
  • He received the Ellis Island Medal of Honor in 2012
