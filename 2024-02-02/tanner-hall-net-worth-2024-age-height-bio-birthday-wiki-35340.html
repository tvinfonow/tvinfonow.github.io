Top Facts about Tanner Hall

1. Tanner Hall (born November 24, 1982) is an American professional skier.
2. He is the founder and head coach of the Big Mountain Ski and Snowboard Camp in Whitefish, Montana.
3. He is a five-time X Games Gold Medalist in the Ski Slopestyle event, and the 2009 AFP World Champion in SkierX.
4. Tanner has also won 10 awards from the International Freeskiers and Snowboarders Awards.
5. During his professional career, Hall has been featured in six ski films, including “The Way I See It” and “Days of My Youth”.
6. He is also the owner of Tanner Hall Pro Models with Armada Skis, Smith Optics, Gatorade and more.
7. He has also founded his own clothing brand called “T.Hall”, which is sold in several countries.
8. He won three gold medals at the Winter X Games in 2004, 2006 and 2007.
9. In 2008, he suffered a career-threatening injury after jumping off a giant cliff in Chamonix, France and tearing his ACL, MCL and PCL.
10. He had to undergo several surgeries and an extensive rehabilitation period to recover from the injury.
11. After the injury, he changed his focus from freestyle skiing to backcountry skiing.
12. In 2011, Hall became the first skier to ever ski a 4,000-foot line at Mount St. Elias in Alaska.
13. In 2012, he was honored as “International Skier of the Year” by Powder Magazine.
14. He is the author of the book My Rules: Stories, Reflections, and Life Lessons, which he published in 2019.
15. He has helped to launch several charities, including the Tanner Hall Foundation to help disadvantaged youth experience the outdoors.
