Teddy Riley was raised in St. Nicholas Houses in Harlem. A child prodigy from the age of five, he began playing instruments in church. His uncle, who owned the famed Harlem club The Rooftop, built a studio in the club in which Riley would spend most of his time while growing up. By 14, upstate New York rappers began making music to his tracks. Under the guidance of local music producer Gene Griffin, Riley formed the short-lived group Kids at Work. At the age of 18, Riley produced Kool Moe Dee’s 12″ single, “Go See the Doctor”. Released on an independent label in 1986, the song became a crossover hit, reaching No.89 on the Billboard Hot 100. Riley had previously worked on the production of Doug E. Fresh and the Get Fresh Crew’s “The Show” in 1985.

In 1987, Riley, Aaron Hall, and Timmy Gatling formed the R&B group Guy. Managed by Gene Griffin, Riley’s work with Guy pioneered the “new jack swing” style of R&B. Riley infused his own blend of hip-hop beats, R&B progressions and the gospel vocal stylings of Hall to create the archetypal new jack swing sound on Guy’s eponymous debut. In 1989, Riley produced Big Daddy Kane’s “I Get The Job Done”, as well as other work for The Jacksons, The Winans and James Ingram. He also created the highly successful remix of Jane Child, “Don’t Wanna Fall in Love”, which became a crossover pop smash.
