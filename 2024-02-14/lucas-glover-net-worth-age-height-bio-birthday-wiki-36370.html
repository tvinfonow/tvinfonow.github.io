Lucas Glover Biography

Lucas Glover is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on November 12, 1979 in Greenville, South Carolina, United States. Glover won by two strokes the U.S. Open at Bethpage Black Course in Farmingdale (New York), beating Phil Mickelson and Ricky Barnes. The event was won by Glover, who was ranked 71st on the Official World Golf Ranking. He also had never made the cut in any of his previous U.S. Open appearances. Glover was among a few players to win the U.S. Open, after having played in a sectional qualifying event. Glover shot a 64 in the second round, which tied him for the lowest round of the week with Mike Weir. Glover was one stroke behind Ricky Barnes, 54-hole leader. However, Glover and Barnes struggled in the tough conditions of Monday’s final round. Glover was at the 16th hole with a 4 over par total, but this would change soon. His approach shot was within 6 feet of the hole. He made the putt to claim a lead that he wouldn’t relinquish. Glover won the 18th hole by two strokes after he putted a 3 foot par putt. He moved up to 18th place in the world rankings after his win.

Glover’s breakthrough year was 2003 on the Nationwide Tour. Glover made 17 of 26 cuts, while achieving six top-10 finishes and seven top-25 finishes. At the Gila River Classic at Wild Horse Pass Resort, he won his first Nationwide Tour victory. Glover finished 17th on the moneylist with $193,989. Glover’s high ranking on the money list earned his PGA Tour card in 2004.

Despite not winning an event in 2006, Glover played well on tour. He made 23 of 31 cuts and finished in the top-10 9 times and the top-25 17 times. His most impressive finish came at the WGC-Bridgestone Invitational where he finished in a tie for fourth. Glover earned $2,587,982 in 2006 and finished 31st on the money list. He was ranked in and out of the top 50 of Official World Golf Rankings during the year.

2007 was another good year for Glover. He made the cut in 22 of 29 events and recorded three top-10s and 13 top-25s. His best finish came at the PODS Championship where he finished in a tie for fourth. Glover earned $1,664,167 on the year and finished 53rd on the money list. He was selected for the 2007 Presidents Cup where the United States defeated the International team 19½ to 14½. Glover went 2–3 in his matches.

After graduating from Clemson, Glover became a professional in 2001. In 2002, Glover joined the Nationwide Tour. He made 7 of 12 cuts and recorded two top-10 finishes. His rookie season saw him finish fifth in the top 25. He was 67th on the money-list and earned $64,692. Glover attended PGA Tour qualifying school, where he tied for 39th. Lucas Hendley Glover, a professional American golfer, was born November 12, 1979. He is most well-known for his victory in the 2009 U.S. Open.

Glover was a student at Clemson University between 1998 and 2001. He was also a brother to Alpha Tau Omega. In 2000 and 2001, he was first-team All-American. He was also honorable mention All-American for 1999. He was part of the 2001 United States Walker Cup Team and the 1999 USA-Japan Cup Team. He won three college tournaments, and also won the South Carolina Amateur 1998, 1999, and 2000 as well as the Sunnehanna Amateur 2001.

In his rookie season on tour, Glover made 17 of 30 cuts while recording two top-10s and five top-25s. His best finishes came at the Wachovia Championship and the FUNAI Classic at the Walt Disney World Resort where he finished in a tie for 10th. He earned $557,454 and finished 134th on the money list, just shy of the top 125 which would earn him his tour card for 2005.
