Lewis was selected during the 2nd round (66th overall) and was assigned to the short-season-A Salem-Keizer Volcanoes. There, he the team earned Northwest League All-Star honors, leading the league with the league’s highest .322 in batting average. He also placing 5th in the number of hits (77) as well as at-bat percentage(.396). Lewis was also named Co-Player of the Month for July, alongside Dan Ortmeier and Greg Bruso.

Lewis was a full-time player in The Hagerstown Suns, where he ended the season in second place within the Giants organization, with 30 bases stolen. Lewis also was the top player on the team with scores of 61 runs, being third on the team in both the games (114) as well as doubles(17). Lewis ended the season with an .250 in batting, but because he led his team with walks, with 68, ended up with an .361 percent on base.

Lewis made his first appearance on a Major League 25-man roster on May 6, 2007, and hit for the cycle on May 13, Mother’s Day, in only his 16th game in the majors at Colorado. Lewis doubled leading off the game, then hit a three-run opposite-field home run in the fourth inning (both against Taylor Buchholz), followed by an RBI triple in the fifth (against Tom Martin) and a single leading off the seventh inning (against Denny Bautista). Lewis’ cycle was the 22nd in Giants franchise history. Lewis is one of only a handful of Major League baseball players to hit his first homer as part of a cycle.

On April 26, 2008, Lewis hit the 46th Splash Hit at AT&T Park. Lewis led the majors in steals of home in 2008 with 2, but was also caught twice while attempting to steal home.

After his graduation in 2001, Lewis was selected into the 20th round (585th overall) by the Montreal Expos, but opted not to sign with them, instead, he enrolled in Southern University in Baton Rouge, Louisiana to play baseball. While at Southern University, Lewis flipped the truck he was driving during a stormy night and three occupants inside the vehicle were killed.
