Bofill is a native of the Brooklyn neighborhood located in New York City to a Cuban father and a Puerto Rican mother. Growing up on the streets of The Bronx, Bofill grew in a culture of Latin music and was attracted by African-American musicians. In Bofill’s early years her weekends were filled by studying classical music and performing in a city chorus. In High school, Bofill attended Hunter College High School and graduated in 1972. Bofill continued to study in the Manhattan School of Music, receiving a Bachelor of Music degree in 1976.

Bofill was the first professional to begin her singing career throughout her teenage years. Bofill sang with Ricardo marrero and the Group as well as Dance Theater of Harlem chorus before being introduced Dave Grusin and Larry Rosen of the jazz label GRP Records by Dave Valentin her jazz flutist friend. Grusin as well as Rosen were signed by Bofill and recorded her debut album Angie which was released in the year 1978. Angie was well-received both commercially and critically.
