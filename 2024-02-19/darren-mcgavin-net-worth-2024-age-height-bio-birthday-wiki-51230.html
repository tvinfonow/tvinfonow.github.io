Top Facts about Darren McGavin

  • Darren McGavin was born William Lyle Richardson on May 7, 1922, in Spokane, Washington.
  • He originally studied architecture at the University of the Pacific and theatre at the HB Studio in New York City.
  • He is best known for his role as Mike Hammer in the 1958–1959 television series, Mickey Spillane’s Mike Hammer.
  • In 1955, he starred in Summertime and The Man with the Golden Arm.
  • He became a household name and received much critical acclaim for his role in Riverboat, from 1959 to 1961.
  • He also starred as Carl Kolchak in the 1974 television series Kolchak: The Night Stalker.
  • In 1976, he appeared in the film No Deposit, No Return and the following year starred in Airport ’77.
  • He portrayed “the Old Man” in Bob Clark’s A Christmas Story in 1983.
  • He was nominated for an Emmy Award for his recurring guest-star role in the sitcom Murphy Brown.
  • McGavin starred as Oliver Spencer in the original pilot film for The Six Million Dollar Man in 1973.
  • He directed the film Happy Mother’s Day, Love George in 1973.
  • He appeared as Mark Troxel on The Virginian in 1964.
  • He was cast as the lead in the supernatural-themed television film The Night Stalker in 1972.
  • He narrated a number of audiobooks, notably the Robert Ludlum Jason Bourne Trilogy and John D. MacDonald’s Travis McGee series.
  • He was a staunch liberal and an active Democrat.
