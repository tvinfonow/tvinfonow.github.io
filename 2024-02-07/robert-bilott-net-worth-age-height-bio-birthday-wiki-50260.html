Robert Bilott Biography

Robert Bilott is one of the most popular and richest Attorney who was born on August 2, 1965 in Ohio, United States.

Robert Bilott (born August 2, 1965) is an American environmental attorney from Cincinnati, Ohio. Bilott is known for the lawsuits against DuPont on behalf of plaintiffs from West Virginia. Bilott has spent more than twenty years litigating hazardous dumping of the chemicals Perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) and Perfluorooctanesulfonic acid (PFOS).

Bilott was admitted to the Bar in 1990 and began his law practice at Taft Stettinius & Hollister LLP in Cincinnati, Ohio For eight years he worked almost exclusively for large corporate clients and his specialty was defending chemical companies. He became a partner at the firm in 1998.

In response, DuPont advised that DuPont and the United States Environmental Protection Agency would commission a study of the farmer’s property, conducted by three veterinarians chosen by DuPont and three chosen by the Environmental Protection Agency. In August 2001, Bilott filed a class action lawsuit against DuPont, which was settled in September 2004, with DuPont agreeing to install filtration plants in the six affected water districts and pay a cash award of $70 million. After a scientific panel paid for by DuPont found that there was a probable link between drinking PFOA and kidney cancer, testicular cancer, thyroid disease, high cholesterol, pre-eclampsia, and ulcerative colitis, Bilott began opening personal-injury lawsuits against DuPont on behalf of affected citizens of West Virginia, which by 2015 numbered over 3,500. After winning the first three for $19.7M, in 2017 DuPont agreed to settle the remainder for $671.7 million.

In 2018, Bilott filed a class action that “seeks relief on behalf of a nationwide class of everyone in the United States” against 3M, DuPont, and Chemours. His litigation against DuPont and 3M is ongoing as of May 2019.
