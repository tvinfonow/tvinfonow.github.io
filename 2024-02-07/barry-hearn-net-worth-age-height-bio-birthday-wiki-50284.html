Top Facts about Barry Hearn

1. Barry Maurice William Hearn was born in 1948 in Dagenham, Essex.
2. He was educated at Buckhurst Hill County High School.
3. Hearn began promoting sporting events in 1974, working with amateur snooker players Geoff Foulds and Vic Harris.
4. Hearn prospered from the snooker boom of the 1980s when he formed Matchroom with players Steve Davis and Tony Meo.
5. Hearn took over a 51% controlling interest in the World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association (WPBSA) in 2010.
6. He began promoting boxing in 1987, with the Frank Bruno vs. Joe Bugner bout in White Hart Lane.
7. Hearn has since promoted many leading British and Irish boxers, including Chris Eubank, Nigel Benn, Lennox Lewis, Naseem Hamed, and Herbie Hide.
8. Hearn introduced the Prizefighter series in 2008, a knockout tournament featuring 8 different boxers.
9. Hearn was the chairman of Leyton Orient F.C. from 1995 to 2014.
10. Hearn was appointed Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) in the 2021 New Year Honours for services to sport.
11. He was inducted into the Professional Darts Corporation Hall of Fame in 2021.
12. Hearn is an enthusiastic fisherman, and pitched the idea of Fish-o-Mania to Sky Sports.
13. Married to Susan, the couple have two children, Katie and Eddie Hearn.
14. After nearly going bankrupt in the early 1990s, and suffering a heart attack in 2002, Hearn returned and has become a multi-millionaire.
15. Hearn is the founder and President of promotions company Matchroom Sport, and is involved in many sports including snooker, darts, pool, tenpin bowling, golf, table tennis, and fishing.
