Naseem Shah Biography

Naseem Shah is one of the most popular and richest Cricketer who was born on February 15, 2003 in Lower Dir District, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan. Naseem Shah (Pashto, nsym bs hh ; born on 15 February 2003) was a Pakistani cricketer. At 16 years old, he was selected for the Pakistan cricket team’s Test series against Australia.

In November 2019, he made his international debut against Australia for Pakistan, becoming the ninth-youngest player in Test cricket. He became the second-youngest and youngest pace bowler to record a five-wicket win in a Test match against Sri Lanka in December 2019.

He was initially named in Pakistan’s squad for the 2020 Under-19 Cricket World Cup, but after his impressive start in Test cricket, he was withdrawn from the U19 team. In January 2020, the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) issued a statement on Naseem being withdrawn from their U19 squad. They reiterated that it was due to cricketing reasons, and not concerns of his age, stating they have checked and verified the ages of all cricketers who could play in their U19 team.

In February 2020, in the first Test against Bangladesh, he became the youngest bowler to take a hat-trick in a Test match.

He was selected in Central Punjab’s squad to play in the 2019-20 Quaid-e-Azam trophy tournament. He was named by the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB), as one of six players to be on the radar ahead of the 2019-20 National T20 Cup tournament. On 13 October 2019, he made his Twenty20 debut for Central Punjab in 2019-20 National T20 Cup.
