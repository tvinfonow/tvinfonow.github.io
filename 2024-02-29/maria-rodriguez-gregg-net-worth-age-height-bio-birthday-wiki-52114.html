Maria Rodriguez-Gregg Biography

Maria Rodriguez-Gregg is one of the most popular and richest Politician who was born on September 29, 1981 in Fort Carson, Colorado, United States. Maria Rodriguez-Gregg (born September 29th 31st, 1981) is an American Republican Party politician who was a member of the New Jersey General Assembly from 2014 until 2018, and represented in the 8th Legislative District. She was elected to succeed Scott Rudder to become the first Republican Hispanic woman to be elected to the New Jersey Legislature.

Rodriguez-Gregg has always maintained her innocence. There was no alcohol or marijuana discovered by police officers in her vehicle. According to her lawyer, Rodriguez-Gregg’s blood tests indicated “some indication of alcohol” or an “minimal” presence of alcohol however, there was no marijuana. On December 17, 2017, the judge dismissed Rodriguez-Gregg’s motions for suppression of the evidence gathered by the police and to dismiss any charges she was facing.

On April 28, 2017, Rodriguez-Gregg was arrested following a traffic accident in which her car, while stopped at a traffic light, was struck in the rear bumper by another vehicle. Rodriguez-Gregg was charged with driving while intoxicated (marijuana and/or alcohol), obstruction of justice (for refusing a field sobriety test), reckless driving, and a marked lanes violation. The other driver was charged with careless driving. Both drivers were treated for minor injuries.

In January 2018, Rodriguez-Gregg claimed that she was a survivor of domestic abuse that had been occurring at the time of her arrest. In a Facebook post and several media interviews, Rodriguez-Gregg shared details of her alleged abuse and photographs of her face with multiple bruises, and urged support for pending legislation that strengthened penalties for domestic violence. In response, some local media outlets described Rodriguez-Gregg as “courageous” for publicly discussing her domestic violence abuse.

The 2016 United States Presidential election, Rodriguez-Gregg was the state’s sole Republican Hispanic state lawmaker, refused to support Republican Presidential nominee Donald Trump and stated, “I think it’s my whole life experience that leads me to not being able to support him.”
