Lloyd James Austin III, born August 8, 1953, is a former Four-Star General in the United States Army. He was the 12th Commander of United States Central Command. Austin was the first black commander of the organization. Austin was the 33rd Vice-Chairman of the United States Army, serving from January 31, 2012 to March 8, 2013, before he was assigned to CENTCOM. Prior to CENTCOM, Austin was the 33rd Vice Chief of Staff of United States Army from January 31, 2012 to March 8, 2013. The Pentagon announced on December 6, 2012 that President Barack Obama had appointed Austin to head the U.S. Central Command. On March 5, 2013, the U.S. Senate confirmed Austin and he assumed command on March 22, 2013. Austin retired from the military on April 5, 2016. He was awarded the Defense Distinguished Service Medal and the Army Distinguished Service Medal. The Defense Superior Service Medal, Defense Superior Service Medal, and the Legion of Merit were also presented to him.

Austin was sent to Indianapolis, Indiana in 1981 as the Operations Officer of the U.S. Army Indianapolis District Recruiting Command. Austin served from September 2003 until August 2005 as the Commanding General of the 10th Mountain Division (Light Infantry), with duty as Commander, Combined Joint Task Force 180, during the War in Afghanistan.
