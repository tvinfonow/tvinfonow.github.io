Rudolph Contreras, born December 6, 1962, is a United States District judge of the United States District Court for the District of Columbia. He is also a United States Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court judge.

On July 28, 2011, President Barack Obama appointed Contreras as the United States District Court for the District of Columbia’s replacement for Judge Ricardo M. Urbina. Urbina was elevated to senior status in 2011. The Senate Judiciary Committee held an October 4th hearing to consider Contreras’ nomination. On October 3, 2011, the Senate floor reported his nomination. Contreras was confirmed by the Senate in a voice vote on March 22, 2012. On March 23, 2012, he was appointed to his new position.

In 2017 Contreras was randomly assigned the case of United States of America v. Michael T. Flynn, the former national security adviser to President Donald Trump. The two-page indictment was released on December 1, 2017. Contreras accepted Flynn’s guilty plea to one count of making false statements to the FBI in the course of their investigation into Russian interference in the 2016 U.S. presidential election. The guilty plea was part of a plea bargain with the Special Counsel investigation led by Robert S. Mueller III. On December 7, 2017, Contreras was recused from further sentencing hearings scheduled to take place in the future. The case was randomly reassigned to District Judge Emmet G. Sullivan.
