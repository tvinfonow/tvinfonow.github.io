Henry Cuellar Biography

Henry Cuellar is one of the most popular and richest Politician who was born on September 19, 1955 in Laredo, Texas, United States. Cuellar was born and raised in Laredo in South Texas. He has lived there most of his adult life. Martin Siller Cuellar Sr., his Mexican-American parents (1926-1999), and Odilia Perez (1928-1915), were both born in Laredo. Odilia, a native of Zapata (Texas), traveled to Idaho every year as migrant laborer until Martin was hired as a ranch manager and gardener. The Cuellar family, which included eight children, lived on Reynolds Street in “The Heights” Laredo. Although the parents did not speak English, they instilled in their children a strong work ethic.

Cuellar represented Laredo’s major part of Texas House of Representatives, from 1987 to 2001. Cuellar was Laredo’s state representative for 14 years. He held leadership positions on the House Appropriations and Higher Education committees. He was also a member of several national legislative panels that dealt with the state budgets, U.S./Mexico border and international trade.

In 2001, Cuellar was appointed as Secretary of State of Texas by Republican Governor Rick Perry, serving in the position for eight months.

In the 2002 election, Cuellar was the Democratic nominee for the House of Representatives in Texas’s 23rd congressional district. He lost to five-term incumbent Republican Henry Bonilla by 52–47 in the closest race Bonilla had faced up to that date. Bonilla was unseated in 2006 in the revised 23rd District by the liberal Democrat Ciro Rodriguez.

Cuellar started his own law practice in Laredo in 1982 and was licensed as a customs broker in 1983. From 1984 to 1986, he was an adjunct professor in International Commercial Law at Texas A&M International University.

Cuellar challenged Rodriguez, a former friend, for the nomination and won it by fifty-eight votes.
