Brian Harman Biography

Brian Harman is one of the most popular and richest American professional golfer who was born on January 19, 1987 in Savannah, Georgia, United States. Brian Harman (born January 19 1987) is an American professional golfer in the PGA Tour. He is among the few players who are left-handed on the tour.

In 2010 Harman was a regular player during his EGolf Professional Tour finishing in the top 10 in 11 of his fourteen starts. Harman scored his first professional triumph in Manor Classic. Manor Classic where he won by three shots. He also participated at three Nationwide Tour events in 2010. His most impressive performance came during the Stadion Athens Classic at UGA which he finished T-18th on his old college course.

Harman was known for a unique situation at the 2012 Players Championship. He was the first alternate when D. A. Points withdrew just minutes before his tee time. Playing partners Carl Pettersson and Robert Garrigus had already teed off and after consulting with the PGA, tournament officials allowed Harman to tee off alone for the first round. Harman eventually got partnered with Ryan Moore and Bud Cauley for round two after Paul Casey withdrew. Harman made the cut and finished T51.

On August 30, 2015, at The Barclays at Plainfield Country Club in Edison, New Jersey, Harman became the third player in PGA Tour history to have two aces in the same round.

In the college years, Harman was a three-time 2nd Team All-American on the University of Georgia golf team. He was the winner of in 2005 the NCAA Preview and the 2006 Isleworth Invitational. He also took home the annual award for the highest GPA three times.
