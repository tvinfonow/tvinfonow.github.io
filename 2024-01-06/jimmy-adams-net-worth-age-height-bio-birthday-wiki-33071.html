Jimmy Adams Biography

Jimmy Adams is one of the most popular and richest Jamaican cricketer who was born on January 9, 1968 in Port Maria, Saint Mary Parish, Jamaica. James Clive Adams (born 9 January 1968) was an ex- Jamaican cricketer who played for Jamaica in the West Indies as player and captain during his time. He was a consistent left-handed batsman, a useful left-handed spin bowler who was orthodox and a an excellent fielder, especially at the position of the gully. He was also a frequent wicketkeeper when needed. He was head coach of Kent County Cricket Club for five seasons from 2012 to October 2016.

He resigned from all cricket in 2004 following his twenty-year playing career that ended with a Test batting percentage of 41.26 and a record-breaking number of 208 not-outs in the match against New Zealand at St. John’s, Antigua and Barbuda in 1995.

Adams was appointed West Indies captain in 2000 to replace Brian Lara. Adams would not stay long as he led his team in a series 5-0 loss during the 2000/01 tour of Australia during the tour, was over, he lost both the captaincy (to Carl Hooper) and his spot as a member of the team. His imminent departure was announced to Adams by a friend of the national TV reporter Peter Furst. Adams simply replied, “Have you heard something I haven’t?” Adams then thought about his professional life, saying that whatever had happened, it was all positive – both good and the bad.
