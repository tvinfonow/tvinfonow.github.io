Top Facts about Henri Young

  • Henri Theodore Young was born in Kansas City, Missouri on June 20, 1911.
  • He was a convicted bank robber and murderer.
  • He attempted to escape from Alcatraz Federal Penitentiary with four other inmates in 1939.
  • Two of the inmates, Dale Stamphill and Arthur “Doc” Barker, were shot, the latter fatally.
  • Young blamed Rufus McCain for the failed attempt at escape.
  • Young was sentenced to solitary confinement at Alcatraz for three years.
  • Eleven days after re-entering the Alcatraz general prison population in 1942, Young murdered McCain.
  • Young’s defense put the spotlight on Alcatraz and the penal system, leading to questions about how the prison was run.
  • In 1948, Young was transferred to the Medical Center for Federal Prisoners in Springfield, Missouri.
  • Upon completion of his federal sentence, he was transferred to Washington State Penitentiary at Walla Walla.
  • He “jumped parole” in 1972 and his whereabouts were reported as “unknown”.
  • Young was portrayed as a fictional character with the same name in the film “Murder in the First” (1995).
  • Young is also the subject of the song “Behind the Eyes of Henri Young” featured on the 2017 album “Dragonfly” by Kasey Chambers.
  • Young began a life of crime and was known for aggressively taking hostages.
