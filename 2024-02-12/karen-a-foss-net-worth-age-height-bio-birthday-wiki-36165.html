Foss’s most recent research has been on the ways feminist perspectives can change the field of communication. She aims to develop and transform theories to offer an expanded toolkit for communicators that does not depend solely on the speech practices of elite white males. Her argument is stated in “Transforming Rhetoric through Female Reconstruction as a Response to the Gender Diversity Perspective” (with Sonja K. Foss and Cindy L. Griffin). She also outlined the field of communication, in feminist rhetorical theories (with Sonja K. Foss and Cindy L. Inviting Transformation Communication for a New World (with Sonja K. Foss) is another example of her efforts to rethink the way we think about public speaking. Foss and Foss present a fresh model for public speaking, which incorporates the principles of invitational rhetoric, as well as speech practices of the marginalized. With Gender Stories (with Sonja K. Foss and Mary E. Domenico), Foss reconsiders gender, sex and feminism as constructs of social society. Foss also juxtaposes the concept of persuasion — the predominant view of change within the discipline–with an alternative view derived from different disciplines and tradition.

Certain of Foss’s works provide brief overviews of rhetorical and communications theories. Current perspectives on Rhetoric (with Sonja K.
