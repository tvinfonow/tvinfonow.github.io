Megan Norma McNamara, a Canadian beach volleyball player, was born August 1, 1997 in Richmond. Megan, along with her twin sister Nicole McNamara has participated in numerous beach volleyball events all over the globe. Megan played collegiate volleyball for the UCLA Bruins from the fall 2015 to the spring 2019. She led the team to the first Pac-12 and NCAA titles during the 2018 season and the second NCAA championship in 2019.

Megan and Nicole went 28-7 in dual matches during the 2017 season. This led to the Bruins a record of 30-6 overall. After losing to Florida State in an Elimination Bracket match, the team was runner-up in the Pac-12 Beach Volleyball Championships. However, they were defeated in the NCAA tournament in their third match. She and Nicole were named Pac-12 Pairs of the Week during the season. Megan was awarded All-Pac-12 First Team honors and AVCA All-American honors, just like her freshman year. After her second year in the classroom, she was named to the Pac-12 All-Academic first team for beach volleyball.

During the 2018 season, Megan and Nicole went 35-7 in dual matches, leading the UCLA Bruins to a 40-4 overall record and to their first Pac-12 and NCAA championships, beating USC and Florida State, respectively. Once again, the sisters were honored as members of the All-Pac-12 First Team, and as All-Americans in the process. Additionally, the McNamara twins were named Pac-12 Pair of the Year in the week following completion of the championship. Following the season, Megan was once again named to the Pac-12 All-Academic First Team to honor her performance in the classroom for her junior year.

During the 2019 season, Megan and Nicole went 32-4 in dual matches, leading the UCLA Bruins to a 35-3 overall record and their second consecutive NCAA championship, beating the USC in the final match. Additionally, they finished runner-up to USC at the Pac-12 championship. One final time in their collegiate careers, both sisters were honored as members of the All-Pac-12 First Team and as AVCA Collegiate All-Americans. Additionally, the McNamara twins were named Pac-12 and AVCA/CollegeBeachVB.com Pair of the Week for the week of April 2.

Megan and Nicole won bronze at the 2016 FIVB Beach volleyball U21 World Championships. They came back from one set down (18-21), 23-21, 15, 10-10) against Sarah Sponcil of the USA and Torrey van Winden in the third-place match.
