Corey Conners (born January 6 1993) was an Canadian professional golfer who is currently for the PGA Tour. Conners has been a part of PGA Tour Canada, PGA Tour Latinoamerica and the Web.com Tour.

In March of 2018, Conners held the 54-hole lead at the Valspar Championship in Florida, one stroke clear on Justin Rose, Brandt Snedeker and Tiger Woods. The fourth time around, Conners shot 77 and ended up finishing T16. Conners finished the season 130th on the FedEx Cup, five spots away from full status for the season of 2019.

In October of 2018, Conners finished second to Cameron Champ in the PGA Tour’s Sanderson Farms Championship in Jackson, Mississippi, winning $475,200.

Corey Conners Net Worth

Corey Conners is one of the richest Golfer from Canada. According to our analysis, Wikipedia, Forbes & Business Insider, Corey Conners's net worth $5 Million. (Last Update: December 11, 2023)

Conners was born from Listowel, Ontario. Conners won his first title in the Ontario Amateur and was runner-up to Gunn Yang at the 2014 U.S. Amateur, which was held by the Atlanta Athletic Club. Conners was a participant at the 2014 Masters Tournament where he missed the cut. He made the switch to professional play immediately afterward and also played for the RBC Canadian Open in 2016.

In December of 2016, Conners tied for 42nd at the Web.com Tour qualifying tournament. In one of the top forty-five, he was able to earn an invitation to the Web.com Tour in 2017.

On April 7, 2019, Conners won the PGA Tour’s Valero Texas Open by two strokes over Charley Hoffman, earning the final place in the 2019 Masters Tournament. Due to his conditional status, Conners had to play in a Monday qualifying event, where he earned the final spot after a six-man playoff. He became the first Monday qualifier to win on the PGA Tour since Arjun Atwal at the 2010 Wyndham Championship. The first place prize was $1,350,000. Conners finished the 2019 regular season in 31st place. He finished 21st at The Northern Trust and 7th at the BMW Championship, which earned him a spot at the Tour Championship.
