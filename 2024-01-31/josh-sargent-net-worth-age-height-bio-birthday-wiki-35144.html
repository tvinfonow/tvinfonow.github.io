Josh Sargent Biography

Josh Sargent is one of the most popular and richest Association Football Player who was born on February 20, 2000 in O'Fallon, Missouri, United States. Joshua Thomas Sargent (born February 20th in 2000) is an American soccer player who is a forward in Werder Bremen in the Bundesliga as well as for the United States national soccer team. In May 2017, at 17 years old, Sargent was the youngest U.S. player to score at the FIFA U-20 World Cup.

Two days after, Sargent was surprisingly included by coach Tab Ramos in the 21-man team named to represent the United States at the 2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup. On May 22nd the Americans began their match against Ecuador. Sargent began the game and scoring two goals, assisting his team win the match and becoming the youngest American player to score at the U20 World Cup. Sargent was the only U.S. player (after Freddy Adu) to participate in both the U-17 as well as the U-20 World Cups in the same year. On June 1st, 2017, following making a goal on the road against New Zealand, Sargent joined Jozy Altidore, Eddie Johnson and Taylor Twellman and became among the U.S.’s most prolific goal scorers in the U-20 World Cup.

On September 20, 2017, Werder Bremen announced that Sargent would join the club on January 1, 2018 and then sign a professional contract on his 18th birthday, per FIFA rules. He played with the club’s under-23 squad in a friendly match and signed his contract on February 20, 2018, making him eligible to join the first team for the 2018–19 season.

On December 7, 2018, Sargent made his debut for the Werder Bremen first team coming on as a substitute in the 76th minute in a match against Fortuna Düsseldorf. He scored with his first touch of the match in the 78th minute, the fastest goal by a debutant in club history.

On the 7th of November, 2017 Sargent was the first player to receive a senior call-up to an friendly match against Portugal. This made him just the one American player ever to appear in the U-17, U-20, and MNT camp within that same year. On May 28, 2018, Sargent earned his first cap for the senior squad in a friendly match against Bolivia in which he scored the first goal of his international career. Sargent became the fourth-youngest athlete scoring for United States.
