Lise Davidsen Biography

Lise Davidsen is one of the most popular and richest Singer who was born on February 8, 1987 in Stokke Municipality, Norway. Lise Davidsen was 15 years old when she began singing and playing guitar. She began to focus on singing and was awarded a bachelor’s degree in 2010 from the Grieg Academy of Music, Bergen, Norway. She sang with the Norwegian Soloists Choir as a mezzo-soprano and worked with famous singers like Bettina Smith and Hilde Hartaldsen.

She won first prize in 2015’s Queen Sonja Competition, and first prize and audience prizes in the Operalia competition. She was also awarded three prizes in the 2015 Hans Gabor Belvedere singing competition in Amsterdam. In 2018, she was awarded the Queen Ingrid Prize, and was named Gramophone Magazine Young Artists of the Year.

Davidsen has recorded two albums. In 2016, she recorded songs by John Frandsen, on the Dacapo Records label, and later that year her record As Dreams, on BIS Records, featured her with the Norwegian Soloists’ Choir, and the Oslo Sinfonietta, conducted by Grete Pedersen, singing works by Alfred Janson, Helmut Lachenmann, Per Nørgård, Kaija Saariaho, and Iannis Xenakis. She sang the role of Anitra in a recording of Peer Gynt with the Bergen Philharmonic Orchestra and Choir conducted by Edward Gardner on the Chandos label released in 2018. In May 2018 she signed an exclusive recording contract with Decca Classics, and on May 31, 2019, her self-titled debut solo album was released on the label. The album includes works by Strauss and Wagner performed with the Philharmonia Orchestra conducted by Esa-Pekka Salonen.

After graduating from the Royal Opera Academy, she began her Master’s program. Susanna Eken was her teacher and helped her to develop her voice for opera. She was a soloist in the Berlin Philharmonic’s 2014 concert at the Royal Danish Music Conservatory. She graduated from the Royal Opera Academy that year and was awarded the Leonie Sing talent prize and the Danish Singers Award. She was also supported financially by the Skipsreder Tom Wilhelmsen Foundation, Karen and Arthur Feldthusens Foundations, and Sine Butenschons Foundations.
