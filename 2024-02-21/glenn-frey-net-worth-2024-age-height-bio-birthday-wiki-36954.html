Glenn Frey Biography

Glenn Frey is one of the most popular and richest Rock Singer who was born on November 6, 1948 in Detroit, Michigan, United States. Singer-songwriter with the rock band The Eagles, who was the lead vocalist on such hits as “Tequila Sunrise” and “Take It Easy.” He also released five solo albums, with the first two, No Fun Aloud and The Allnighter, earning Gold certification.

He and Don Henley were the two primary songwriters for The Eagles.

He was married to Janie Beggs from 1983 to 1988 and Cindy Millican from 1990 until his death. He and Cindy had three children, Taylor, Deacon, and Otis.
