Victor J. Glover Biography

Victor J. Glover is one of the most popular and richest Astronaut who was born on April 30, 1976 in Prosper, Texas, United States. Victor Jerome Glover (born April 30, 1976) is a NASA astronaut of the class of 2013.

In August 2018 Glover was assigned to fly on the first operational flight, and the second crewed flight overall, of SpaceX’s Crew Dragon to the International Space Station as part of NASA’s Commercial Crew Development Program. As part of that mission, he will be a crew member on ISS Expeditions 64 & 65, making him the first African-American crew member on ISS.

When did Victor Glover go to the ISS?

On Nov. 15, 2020, Glover left Earth as pilot and second-in-command of Crew-1, SpaceX’s first contracted, fully operational astronaut mission to the ISS. Upon his arrival at the space station on Nov. 17, 2020, he became the first Black astronaut to stay for an extended mission in orbit.
