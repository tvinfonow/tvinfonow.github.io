Andersson arrived in New Orleans in 1990 to perform with her fellow musician and singer Swede, Anders Osborne. After nine years, the singer-songwriter resigned from the group. Since the time, Andersson has performed and recorded with a variety of famous New Orleans musicians, including Allen Toussaint, The Neville Brothers, The Meters and Betty Harris. In 2007 she accepted an invitation to be a part of the concert Goin’ Home A Tribute To Fats Domino and was a part of “When The Saints Go Marching In” along with The Preservation Hall Jazz Band. Andersson has appeared on Late Night with Conan O’Brien as well as The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, and has been a performer at Voodoo Festival held in New Orleans.

The idea behind her latest Basin Street Records release was born during a tour of Sweden during which she had a meeting producers Tobias Froberg. Without much prodding, the like-minded Tobias took a flight over to New Orleans in the fall of 2007, when they created Hummingbird, Go! in Andersson’s kitchen. Andersson performed the majority of instruments used on the album except for two duets: one featuring New Orleans producer Allen Toussaint and another with Norwegian singer Ane Brun. This album was recorded and mixed with Linus Larsson (Peter, Bjorn and John, Mercury Rev).
