J. J. Jansen Biography

J. J. Jansen is one of the most popular and richest American football long snapper who was born on January 20, 1986 in Phoenix, Arizona, United States. Jeffrey Richard “J. J.” Jansen (born January 20, 1986) is an American football long snapper for the Carolina Panthers of the National Football League (NFL). He was signed by the Green Bay Packers as an undrafted free agent in 2008. He played college football at Notre Dame.

On February 7, 2016, Jansen was part of the Panthers team that played in Super Bowl 50. In the game, the Panthers fell to the Denver Broncos by a score of 24–10.

On February 17, 2016, the Panthers signed Jansen to a five-year, $5.5 million contract extension.
