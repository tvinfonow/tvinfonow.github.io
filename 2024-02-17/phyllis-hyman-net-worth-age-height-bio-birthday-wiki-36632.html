Top Facts about Phyllis Hyman

  • Key Facts about Phyllis Linda Hyman:
  • Born July 6, 1949 (died June 30, 1995) in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • Her paternal great-grandparents were Ishmael and Cassandra (Cross) Hyman
  • Performed on Broadway in the 1981 musical Sophisticated Ladies, receiving a Theatre World Award and a Tony Award nomination
  • Recorded her first solo album, Phyllis Hyman (1977) for Buddah label
  • Her follow-up album, You Know How to Love Me (1979), peaked at #21 on Billboard Hot 100
  • Recording career went on pause due to problems with her label, Arista
  • Released the album Living All Alone (1986) and Prime of My Life (1991) on Philadelphia International label
  • Hit single Don’t Wanna Change the World (1991) made it to #37 on Billboard Hot 100
  • Recorded James Bond theme song “Never Say Never Again”, but was not used in the film due to contractual issues
  • Posthumous album releases include I Refuse to Be Lonely (1995), One on One (1998), Forever with You (1998), and In Between the Heartaches (2003)
  • Authorized Biography, Strength of a Woman: The Phyllis Hyman Story, was released in 2007
  • Had one marriage (1977-1982) and no children
  • Cousin of actor Earle Hyman, best known for his role on The Cosby Show as Cliff’s father, Russell Huxtable
  • Suffered from bipolar disorder and depression and self-medicated with alcohol and drugs
  • Died by suicide by overdosing on a mixture of Tuinal and vodka in her New York City apartment
