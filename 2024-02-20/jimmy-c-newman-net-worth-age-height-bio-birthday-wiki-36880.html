Top Facts about Jimmy C. Newman

  • Born August 29, 1927 in Louisiana.
  • Signed with Dot Records in 1953.
  • Had first Top 10 hit with “Cry, Cry Darling” in 1954.
  • Inducted into the Grand Ole Opry in 1956.
  • Recorded over a dozen albums for Decca Records.
  • Won CMA’s Male Vocalist of the Year award in 1979.
  • Collaborated with Cajun musician Doug Kershaw on album “Louisiana Man.”
  • Hosted TV show “The Jimmy C Newman Show” on RFD-TV.
  • Died June 21, 2014 at age of 86 from cancer.
  • Remembered as pioneer of Cajun and country music fusion genre.
