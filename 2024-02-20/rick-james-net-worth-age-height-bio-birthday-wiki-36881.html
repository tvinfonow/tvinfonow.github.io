Top Facts about Rick James

  • Rick James was born on February 1, 1948 in Buffalo, New York.
  • He began his music career as a member of the Mynah Birds.
  • His breakthrough album “Street Songs” was released in 1981.
  • James’ hit song “Super Freak” peaked at #16 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.
  • He struggled with drug addiction throughout his career and life.
  • James was convicted of assaulting two women in separate incidents in the late 1990s.
  • He died on August 6, 2004 from heart failure at age 56.
  • James has been sampled by numerous hip-hop artists including MC Hammer and Kanye West.
  • In addition to music, he also acted in films such as “The Last Dragon”.
  • James won a Grammy Award for Best R&B Song for his work on Mary Jane Girls’ “All Night Long”.
