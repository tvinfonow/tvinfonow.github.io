Top Facts about John Cassavetes

  • John Cassavetes was born on December 9, 1929 in New York City.
  • He acted in over 50 films and TV shows.
  • Cassavetes directed and wrote the screenplay for “Faces” (1968).
  • His film “A Woman Under the Influence” (1974) received two Oscar nominations.
  • Cassavetes co-starred with his wife Gena Rowlands in many of his films.
  • He founded an acting workshop that influenced many actors including Peter Falk.
  • Cassavetes died on February 3, 1989 from cirrhosis of the liver.
  • His films are known for their improvisational style and focus on human relationships.
  • The Criterion Collection has released several of his films on DVD/Blu-ray.
  • Cassavetes is considered a pioneer of American independent cinema.
