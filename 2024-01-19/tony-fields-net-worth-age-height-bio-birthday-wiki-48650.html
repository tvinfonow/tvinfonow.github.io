Tony Fields Biography

Tony Fields is one of the most popular and richest Dancer who was born on December 28, 1958 in Stafford, England, United Kingdom.

Fields was born Anthony Dean Campos in Stafford, Kansas. After his parents separated when he was four, his mother remarried, and he took his stepfather’s last name, Fields. He was raised in Davis, California and showed an early aptitude for gymnastics before taking up dance training. He attended Davis High School, graduating in 1977. Fields then went on to college at the Pacific Conservatory of the Performing Arts on scholarship and Roland Dupree Academy of Dance, but left when paid work began to materialize for him.

He moved to Hollywood to pursue his career, and started performing as a backup dancer in Debbie Reynolds’ nightclub act. He then found a job on the Solid Gold television series as a dancer in 1979. Fields would continue with Solid Gold until 1985. During this time period, he also performed in the music video for Queen’s “Body Language”, at Freddie Mercury’s direct request.
