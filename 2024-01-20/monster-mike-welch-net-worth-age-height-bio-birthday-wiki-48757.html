Monster Mike Welch, born June 11, 1979, is an American guitarist, singer, and songwriter. In 2017, 2018, and 2019, he was nominated to the Blues Music Award. To date, Welch has released seven albums and is a member Sugar Ray & the Bluetones.

Welch recorded and performed with Nick Moss and Johnny Winter, Darrell Nulisch, and the Knickerbocker all-stars which included Jimmie Vaughan. Welch also contributed to Fifty Shades of Blue (2015) by Anthony Geraci and Boston Blues All Stars. Also, he was a guest performer for Billy Price’s 2015 album Strong. Welch also continued his work with Sugar Ray Norcia. In October 2016, Welch released Seeing is Believing, which was issued by Severn Records. The act had previously performed at the Hawaii Theatre in Honolulu earlier that year.

In June 2016, Welch and singer Mike Ledbetter took part in a tribute to Otis Rush at the 33rd Chicago Blues Festival. Ledbetter had previously fronted the Nick Moss Band, and the Welch and Ledbetter live collaboration led to them recording an album together. Welch stated “… from the first time I heard Mike, I knew he was the kind of singer I’ve always been trying to play like.” Ledbetter countered, “The way we played off each other was beautiful to me… Everything just fit perfectly.” The album was titled Right Time Right Place and was released by Delta Groove Productions. Right Place Right Time’s track listing included a number of original songs and several cover versions. These latterly comprise the album’s opening track, a cover of Elmore James’ “Cry for Me Baby”; the Willie Dixon penned “I Can’t Stop Baby”; alongside a reworking of the Jerry Leiber and Artie Butler song “Down Home Girl”; plus “Cryin’ Won’t Help You”, which was an old B.B. King song. Ledbetter died at his home in January 2019, aged 33, of complications from epilepsy.

Welch was nominated for a Blues Music Award in 2017, 2018, and 2019. He won the Award for ‘Instrumentalist – Guitar’ in 2019.
