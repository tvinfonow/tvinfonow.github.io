D Smoke Biography

D Smoke is one of the most popular and richest Rapper who was born on October 17, 1985 in United States, United States. Rapper who is recognized for the singles “Want You To,” “Fighter,” and “Beautiful Boy.” In 2019, he was crowned the winner of the first season of Netflix’s hip-hop competition Rhythm + Flow.

He posed for a photo with fellow rapper Casey Veggies in September of 2019.

D Smoke grew up in a musical family with his mother, brothers, and cousin being gospel singers in Inglewood, California. His brother SiR is signed to Top Dawg Entertainment. In 2006, he started a songwriting group called WoodWorks with his brothers and his cousin Tiffany Gouché, who wrote songs for Ginuwine and The Pussycat Dolls, as he is credited for writing “Never” by Jaheim. He was also in a musical trio with his brother called N3D. On May 9, 2006, he released his first album called Producer of the Year. In 2015, he appeared on SiR’s independent album Seven Sundays on the song “You Ain’t Ready”.

In 2019, he was a contestant on the Netflix competition show Rhythm & Flow, and was named the inaugural winner of the three-week series. On October 24, he released his debut EP Inglewood High, a 7-track project including a feature from Tiffany Gouché. A writer from HipHopDX gave the EP a positive review saying he could “utilize multiple flows, deliver introspective storytelling, could rap in Spanish incredibly well and had an ear for quality.” He performed at the 2019 Soul Train Music Awards with SiR. He also appeared on The Game’s album Born 2 Rap on the song “Cross on Jesus Back”.

He was born in the United States.
