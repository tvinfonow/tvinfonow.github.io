Riho Biography

Riho is one of the most popular and richest Professional Wrestler who was born on June 4, 1997 in Shinagawa, Tokyo, Japan. Riho Hime, also known as Li Bu, Hime Riho (born June 4, 1997) is a Japanese professional wrestler, idol, and better known as the ring name Riho. She is currently a member of All Elite Wrestling, where she was the inaugural AEW Women’s World Champion. In addition, she works as a freelancer for independent promotions like World Wonder Ring Stardom where she was the High Speed Champion during her first reign.

Riho made her first appearances at DDT Pro-Wrestling (DDT) the month after. She teamed up with Kenny Omega, Mr. #6, and O.K. to defeat Mr. Strawberry, Muscle Sakai, and O.K. Revolution for the Nihonkai Six-Man Tag Team Championship. Omega left the championship team on July 20. The title was then declared vacant. Riho and Mr. #6 were announced as new champions on the 24th of July. Riho, Mr. #6 and The Great Kojika defeated Hikaru Sato KeisukeIshii and Yoshihiko. They also won the Jiyugaoka Six-Person Tag Team Championships and UWA World Trios Championships. On September 23, Riho, Jun Kasai, Miyako Matsumoto, and Jaki Numazawa defended the Trios Triple Crown Championship. However, Mr. #6 and The Great Kojika lost the title to Hikaru Sato, Michael Nakazawa, and Tomomitsu Mattsunaga in DDT. Riho made an appearance for Union Pro Wrestling in late 2010 and early 2011. She wore a mask and portrayed the role of Black Cherry, Cherry’s storyline nemesis. Black Cherry #2, and Black Cherry #3 were also added to the storyline by Ice Ribbon wrestlers Emi Sakura and Hikari Minami.

On September 23, 2012, Riho officially joined Emi Sakura’s Bangkok, Thailand-based Gatoh Move Pro Wrestling promotion. Riho made her in-ring debut for Gatoh Move on October 7 in Shinjuku, Tokyo, wrestling Emi Sakura to a ten-minute time limit draw. On November 4, Riho changed her ring name from hiragana “りほ” to kanji “里歩”, another way of writing her old ring name. She then went on to lose to Emi Sakura in a main event singles match. In early 2012, Riho began teaming regularly with Hikari Minami, after the latter brought back the Teens concept, producing Teens4 on March 3. On June 16 at Teens6, Riho was defeated by World Wonder Ring Stardom representative Nanae Takahashi in the match she was allowed to choose herself. On September 17, Riho wrestled her final match at the Ice Ribbon Dojo in Saitama, teaming with her hand-picked partner Tsukasa Fujimoto in a tag team match, where they defeated Hiroyo Matsumoto and Hamuko Hoshi, with Riho pinning Hoshi for the win. On September 23 at Ribbon no Kishitachi 2012, Riho was defeated by Aoi Kizuki in her Ice Ribbon farewell match.

Riho was nine years old when she began professional wrestling training under Emi Sakura at her Ice Ribbon Dojo. On May 29, 2006, she made her debut against Nanae Takahashi in Shinagawa. Riho defeated Makoto in her debut for Ice Ribbon on July 25. Riho’s first year of professional wrestling was dominated by matches with Emi Sakura and Hikari Minami. Riho and Seina met in March 2008 for a four-match series that Riho won 3-1.

Emi Sakura trained her and she debuted in the Ice Ribbon promotion at nine years old. She worked for the promotion for six years and became its first Triple Crown Champion. She won the ICEx60 Championship as well as the International Ribbon Tag Team Championship. She was also a DDT Jiyugaoka Six-Person Tag Team member, DDT Nihonkai Six-Man Tag Team member, and UWA World Trio Champion. In July 2010, she unified the three titles, teaming up with Mr. #6 and The Great Kojika. Riho joined Gatoh Move Pro Wrestling in September 2012 after she left Ice Ribbon. Emi Sakura had founded the new promotion. Riho was awarded the IWA Triple Crown Championship in November 2014.

In January 2011, Riho came together with Ice Ribbon’s younger wrestlers, including former associates Chii Tomiya and Hikari Minami, to form the Heisei YTR (Young Traditional Revolution) stable, under the leadership of Makoto. On February 6, Riho and Makoto made it to the finals of the Ike! Ike! Ima, Ike! Ribbon Tag Tournament, before being defeated by Muscle Venus (Hikaru Shida and Tsukasa Fujimoto) in a match that was also contested for the International Ribbon Tag Team Championship. On March 31, Riho made her debut for Smash at Smash.15, where she teamed with Hikari Minami and Tsukushi to defeat Emi Sakura, Makoto and Mochi Miyagi in a six-woman tag team match, scoring the deciding pinfall over Miyagi. The following month, Riho and Minami began producing their own events under the banner of “Teens”, which would focus on spotlighting Ice Ribbon’s younger wrestlers. In August, the alliance between Riho and Makoto was broken, when Makoto announced that she would be leaving Ice Ribbon for Smash. On August 11, Riho interrupted a Smash press conference, where the transfer was made official, by attacking Smash promoter Tajiri and challenging him to a match. The match took place on August 21, during Makoto’s final night in Ice Ribbon, and saw Tajiri pick up the win. On December 25 at RibbonMania 2011, Seina returned to Ice Ribbon, after a two-year hiatus, to wrestle her retirement match against her Riho. Riho won the match in eight minutes and afterwards refereed a three-minute match, where Seina was defeated by her longtime friend Hikari Minami.
