Charles O'Bannon Biography

Charles O'Bannon is one of the most popular and richest American basketball player who was born on February 22, 1975 in Bellflower, California, United States. Charles Edward O’Bannon Sr. (born 22nd February 1975) is an American former professional basketball player. He was a college basketball player as part of the UCLA Bruins. He was a two-time first team All-Conference player for the Pac-10 (now called the Pac-12) and was a part of the team with his brother Ed to assist the Bruins achieve a national title in 1995. O’Bannon was a part of two seasons with the National Basketball Association (NBA) with the Detroit Pistons and also played abroad across Japan, Poland, and Italy.

In the year 2018, O’Bannon was announced as the head trainer of the Seattle Ballers in the Junior Basketball Association (JBA). In the 2018 season, he led Seattle up to an overall record of 6-2 record, and also earned a spot for the 2012 JBA Finals, in which the team fell to the Los Angeles Ballers. Following the conclusion of the inaugural campaign, O’Bannon was named an assistant coach for the supervision of the JBA USA Team (coached by Los Angeles Ballers’ head coach Doyle Balthazer) for their 2018 international tour.

O’Bannon kept his basketball playing career in the professional ranks playing in different teams outside in the United States in Italy, Poland and Japan. He retired in 2013.
