Top Facts about Freddie King

  • Freddie King was an influential American blues guitarist and singer.
  • He was born on September 3, 1934, in Gilmer, Texas.
  • King is known for his powerful guitar playing and soulful vocals.
  • He recorded numerous hits including “Hide Away” and “Have You Ever Loved a Woman.”
  • King’s style combined elements of blues, rock, and funk.
  • He was nicknamed the “Texas Cannonball” due to his explosive performances.
  • King released several successful albums during his career, including “Getting Ready…” and “Burglar.”
  • His guitar playing influenced many notable musicians such as Eric Clapton and Stevie Ray Vaughan.
  • In 2012, he was posthumously inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • Freddie King passed away on December 28, 1976, at the age of 42.
