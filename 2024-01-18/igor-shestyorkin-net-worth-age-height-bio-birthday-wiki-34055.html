Igor Shestyorkin is one of the most popular and richest Celebrity who was born on December 30, 1995 in Moscow, Russia.

Igor Olegovich Shestyorkin (Russian: Игорь Олегович Шестёркин , born 30 December 1995), last name sometimes spelled Shesterkin, is a Russian professional ice hockey goaltender for the New York Rangers of the National Hockey League (NHL). Shestyorkin was a member of the Olympic Athletes from Russia team at the 2018 Winter Olympics. While capturing the Gold medal, he did not see any action throughout the tournament as the nations third goaltender.

On 3 May 2019, Shestyorkin signed a two-year, entry-level contract with the New York Rangers. Shestyorkin played in his first NHL preseason game on 21 September 2019, stopping 36 of 40 shots in a 4–1 loss to the Philadelphia Flyers. He was assigned to the Hartford Wolf Pack, the Rangers’ minor league affiliate team with the American Hockey League. Shestyorkin made his AHL debut during Hartford’s 2019–20 season opener on 5 October 2019, during which he made 26 saves in a 5–3 win against the Charlotte Checkers. On 6 January 2020, the Rangers recalled Shestyorkin after having success for the Wolf Pack with whom he posted a 15–4–3 record and was named to the 2020 AHL All-Star Classic. In his NHL debut with the Rangers on 7 January, Shestyorkin made 29 saves in a 5–3 win against the Colorado Avalanche. Two days later, Shestyorkin became the sixth Rangers’ goaltender to win his first two games with the team.

Shestyorkin made his professional and KHL debut midway through the following 2013–14 season, recalled from MHK to close out the final 8 minutes for Spartak in relief of Alexei Ivanov in a 5–3 defeat to HC Ugra on 20 January 2014. He was given his first start three days later, allowing 2 goals in a 3–1 loss to Lokomotiv Yaroslavl on 23 January 2014. In his eighth appearance, Shestyorkin collected his first win, making 33 saves in a 3–0 shutout victory over HC Slovan Bratislava on 2 March 2014. Returning the MHL, Shestyorkin registered a 12–7 record in 19 playoff games, along with a 1.75 goals against averange and a .937 save percentage, and four shutouts to help MHK Spartak win the MHL Championship.
