Gunhild Carling Biography

Gunhild Carling is one of the most popular and richest Musician who was born on May 7, 1975 in Gothenburg, Västra Götaland, Sweden. She was featured as a singer and multi-instrumentalist in several of Scott Bradlee’s Postmodern Jukebox’s adaptations of pop songs, which include a jazz swing version of Rick Astley’s “Never Going to Give You Up”, a 1920s jazz swing version of “Material Girl”, a vintage jazz version of Europe’s “The Final Countdown”, a jazz cover of Pharrel Williams’ “Happy”, and a 1920s hot jazz cover of ABBA’s “Dancing Queen”.

In 2013 she performed with her “Carling Big Band at The Royal Palace in Stockholm in the celebration of King Carl Gustaf XVI’s Ruby Jubilee. The year was 2016. Carling played at the King’s Carl Gustaf’s birthday party.

In 2018, Carling moved to northern California with her family. She had performances scheduled throughout the United States, but with venues closing due to the COVID-19 pandemic, she often performs live via online social media.

“I haven’t ever been to a music teacher. I come from a family that plays music. I grew up in the south of Sweden, outside of Malmo. Our house was full of variety – circus, acting, dance, vaudeville and novelty. I just picked up instruments from when I was very young and played them. I started with the drums, then the recorder, trombone and trumpet. Then I started tap dancing, and after that harmonica and bagpipe. Later, I began composing music.”

Carling was famous by her appearance in Allsang pa Skansen on July 20th, 2010. The following year, she also became an expert in the field of commentating of Dansbandskampen in SVT. Gunhild performs bagpipes, trombone trumpet, recorder, and string instruments (such as banjo, ukulele, and the harp) and is known to show off all her talents in one song, often simply dancing tap or singing. Carling performed as a famous dancer on Let’s Dance 2014 on TV4 and came in third place.
