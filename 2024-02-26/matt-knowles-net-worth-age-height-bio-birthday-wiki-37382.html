Matt Knowles Biography

Matt Knowles is one of the most popular and richest Association Football Player who was born on October 7, 1970 in Philadelphia, United States. Matt Knowles, born October 7, 1970, is an ex-American soccer defender who played indoor soccer for most of his life. He also played in the American Professional Soccer League (APSL) in the 1990s, and Major League Soccer.

As Major League Soccer (MLS), was just beginning its operations in 1996, Knowles’ indoor success attracted attention. The MetroStars selected Knowles (ninth overall), in the Inaugural Player Draft in February 1996. Knowles signed with MetroStars but continued indoor soccer in the MLS off-season.

In 2001, the Harrisburg Heat picked up Knowles in the dispersal draft. That year, the NPSL disbanded and the surviving NPSL franchises created a new league, the Major Indoor Soccer League (MISL). At some time in 2002, Knowles moved from the Heat to the Kansas City Comets. On October 2, 2002, the Comets traded Knowles to the Philadelphia KiXX in exchange for Matt Caution. However, Knowles again experienced a considerable number of injuries and spent most of the season on the injured-reserved list.

Knowles had great success with the Wave but he was not happy with Wave’s ownership. He expressed a desire for trade. The Wave agreed to Knowles’ wishes and traded him to Cleveland Crunch on December 26, 1996 for Glenn Carbonara and Todd Dusosky and cash. While in Cleveland, he was hurt and did not see much playing time. Knowles signed as a free agent for the Philadelphia KiXX on July 29, 1997 after he purchased the remaining three years from his contract with Crunch. Knowles was awarded the Defender of the Year for the 1997-1998 season. This was his first season in Philadelphia. Knowles was traded to the Detroit Rockers after one more season with KiXX. Knowles was a Detroit Rocker for two seasons before the Rockers folded at end of 2000-2001 season.
