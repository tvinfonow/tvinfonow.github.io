Alice Walker Biography

Alice Walker is one of the most popular and richest Poet who was born on February 9, 1944 in Eatonton, United States. African-American poet and author who is best known for her Pulitzer Prize and National Book Award-winning novel, The Color Purple. Her other literary works include Meridian (1976) and Revolutionary Petunias and Other Poems (1973).

She was inspired by Civil Rights activist, Rosa Parks.

She was born one of seven siblings to Willie Lee Walker and Minnie Lou Tallulah Grant. In 1967, she and Melvyn Roseman Leventhal became the first interracial couple to legally marry in the state of Mississippi. They divorced in 1976. Her daughter, Rebecca Walker, is also a writer.
