Top Facts about Bob Mackie

  • Robert Gordon Mackie is a fashion designer and costumier best known for dressing entertainment icons such as Lucille Ball, Carol Burnett, and Diana Ross.
  • He has received nine Primetime Emmy Awards, a Tony Award, and three nominations for the Academy Award for Best Costume Design.
  • Mackie was born March 24, 1939 in Monterey Park, California and raised in early childhood in Alhambra, California.
  • He earned his first job sketching for Frank Thompson at Paramount Studios and studied under Eva Roberts at Chouinard Art Institute.
  • Mackie was hired by Mitzi Gaynor in 1966 to design her stage show at the Riviera in Las Vegas.
  • He designed costumes for Diana Ross in the television special GIT: On Broadway.
  • Mackie drew the original sketch of Marilyn Monroe’s dress worn in 1962 at President John F. Kennedy’s birthday celebration.
  • Images of many of Mackie’s design drawings for showgirls is available from UNLV Libraries Digital Collections.
  • He designed costumes for Cher’s 2008–11 Las Vegas Cher and 2017 “Classic Cher” shows.
  • Mackie designed Carol Burnett’s ‘curtain dress’ for “Went with the Wind!” sketch on The Carol Burnett Show.
  • He designed the exotic ensemble worn by Cher at the 1986 Academy Awards.
  • Mackie designed costumes for Whitney Houston for her concert tours and award shows.
  • Mackie guest starred as himself on two episodes of The Love Boat in 1981.
  • He was inducted into the Television Hall of Fame in 2002.
  • Mackie won the Tony Award for Best Costume Design in a Musical for The Cher Show in 2019.
