Why was Al Smith important?

Smith was the foremost urban leader of the Efficiency Movement in the United States and was noted for achieving a wide range of reforms as New York governor in the 1920s. Smith was the first Roman Catholic to be nominated for president of the United States by a major party.

What did Al Smith advocate for?

Smith, meanwhile, was the leader of the anti-KKK and anti-prohibition wing of the Democratic Party. Smith stood for cities, immigrants, the repeal of prohibition, religious diversity, and new perspectives on governments role in society.

Why did Al Smith lose the presidential election?

New York State Smith was counting on a win in his home state of New York, which had the most electoral votes of any state.
