Top Facts about Gene Kelly

  • Gene Kelly was born on August 23, 1912 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.
  • He starred in iconic musical films such as Singin’ in the Rain and An American in Paris.
  • Kelly won an Academy Honorary Award for his contributions to film.
  • He served in the US Navy during World War II.
  • Kelly was married three times and had three children.
  • He choreographed many of his own dance routines.
  • Kelly received a Tony Award for Best Choreography for the Broadway production of Red, Hot and Blue!
  • He co-founded the Dance Masters of America organization.
  • Kelly passed away on February 2, 1996 at the age of 83.
  • His legacy continues to inspire dancers and performers today.
