Princess Alexandra of Hanover Biography

Princess Alexandra of Hanover is one of the most popular and richest Figure Skater who was born on July 20, 1999 in Vöcklabruck, Upper Austria, Austria. Alexandra was born in Vocklabruck (Upper Austria) on 20 July 1999. Horst Hirschler of the Evangelical Lutheran Church of Hanover was her christening celebrant. He performed the ceremony at Auerbach, her father’s hunting lodge, near Grunau im Almtal in Upper Austria. Her paternal aunt Alexandra, Princesse of Leiningen, her maternal half-sister Charlotte Casiraghi and Ulrike Ulmschneider are her godparents. Each of her godmothers were named after her.

Alexandra was the first person to attend Monaco’s Rose Ball in March 2015. She was able to attend the ball with her family the following year, and thus gained the attention of the society press.

In October 2018, Princess Alexandra converted to Catholicism, thereby renouncing her distant claim to the British Throne.

Alexandra, Princess Caroline’s only child, does not have any royal titles or styles. Although she is officially styled as Her Royal Honour Princess Alexandra of Hanover, in Monaco, the style and title is given to her out of goodwill. The title is legal recognized in Germany as part her surname, since the Kingdom of Hanover has ended. She is the 13th in line of succession to Monegasque throne. She was in the line to succeed her father to the British throne, until she was confirmed into Catholicism.

Princess Alexandra of Hanover (Alexandra Charlotte Urike Maryam Virginia; 20 July 1999) was the only child of Prince Ernst August and Princess Caroline of Monaco.

Alexandra started skating at the age of ten after she received ice skates for Christmas. She competed in a Toulon competition, France when she was eleven years old. She finished second in the Skate 7 class at the 10th Figure Skating Championships in Monaco when she was 12 years old. She was awarded the Monegasque Federation of Skating cup.

Alexandra represented Monaco in figure skating at the 2015 European Youth Olympic Festival in Austria and in two competitive events during the 2015–16 season at the ISU Junior Grand Prix of Figure Skating.
