Top Facts about Tom Mix

1. Thomas Edwin Mix, born Thomas Hezikiah Mix, was an American film actor and the star of many early Western films between 1909 and 1935.
2. He appeared in 291 films, all but nine of which were silent films.
3. He was born in Mix Run, Pennsylvania on January 6 1880.
4. He worked at the Miller Brothers 101 Ranch, one of the largest ranching businesses in the United States.
5. He and Victoria Forde both signed with Fox Film Corporation in 1918 and had a daughter, Thomasina (Tommie) Mix, in February 1922.
6. He became a Freemason in his lifetime.
7. He stood out as a skilled horseman and expert shot, winning national riding and roping contests at Prescott, Arizona, in 1909, and Canon City, Colorado, in 1910.
8. Mix was a pallbearer at Wyatt Earp’s funeral in January 1929.
9. He was a featured in 11 issues of Dell Comics’ The Comics between 1937 and 1938.
10. The Ralston Purina Company produced nine issues of Tom Mix Comics in 1940–1941, and three issues of Tom Mix Commandos Comics in 1942.
11. Fawcett Comics published 61 issues of Tom Mix Western from 1948 to 1953.
12. He married five times and was divorced four times.
13. He received $40,000 for the four weeks of filming Miracle Rider in 1935.
14. Tom Mix was the acknowledged “King of Cowboys” when Ronald Reagan and John Wayne were young.
15. He has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 1708 Vine Street.

What happened Tom Mix?

On October 12, 1940, cowboy-movie star Tom Mix is killed when he loses control of his speeding Cord Phaeton convertible and rolls into a dry wash (now called the Tom Mix Wash) near Florence, Arizona. He was 60 years old.
