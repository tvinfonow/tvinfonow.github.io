Chris Lewis is one of the most popular and richest Cricketer who was born on February 14, 1968 in Georgetown, Demerara-Mahaica, Guyana. Clairmonte Christopher Lewis (born 14 February 1968) is an English former cricketer which played in Nottinghamshire, Surrey and Leicestershire during the 1990s. He was a part of 33 Test matches and 53 One-Day Internationals (ODIs) for England between 1990 and 1998.

He played four matches in the Newcastle District Cricket Association. Stockton within the Newcastle District Cricket Association in 2007/08.

In 2008, Lewis re-signed for Twenty20 matches for Surrey, in light of his bowling experience. However, his comeback was short and unsuccessful. He played one Friends Provident Trophy game, in which he conceded 51 runs in six overs, and one Twenty20 match. He took no wickets in either.

After a trial at Croydon Crown Court, on 20 May 2009 Lewis and basketball player Chad Kirnon were found guilty and sentenced to 13 years in prison. He was released in June 2015 after serving 6 years of his sentence at HMP High Down.

He was a player in 2006 and was captain of Clifton Cricket Club, in the Central Lancashire Cricket League, in which he was the opening bowling and batting. In his free time, he also taught youngsters in Berkshire.
