Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa Biography

Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa is one of the most popular and richest Sovereign who was born on January 28, 1950 in Riffa, Southern Governorate, Bahrain. Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa, born 28 January 1950 in Riffa (Bahrain). His parents were Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa (then Crown Prince) and Hessa bint Salman Al Khalifa.

Sheikh Hamad began learning to fly helicopters in October 1977. He completed the training in January 1978. The Bahrain Amiri Air Force was established by him in 1987, when the defense force air wing was reconstituted into an air force.

On the death of his father Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa, Hamad became Emir of Bahrain on 6 March 1999. As Emir, Hamad brought several political reforms to Bahrain. These included the release of all political prisoners, the dissolution of the State Security Court and the abolition of the 1974 Decree on State Security Measures. Additionally, many Bahraini citizens were permitted to return after several years in exile overseas. In 2002, he declared himself king. He enjoys wide executive authorities which include appointing the prime minister and his ministers, commanding the army, chairing the Higher Judicial Council, appointing the parliament’s upper half and dissolving its elected lower half.

In October 2010, Hamad’s government accused 24 Shi’ite Bahraini citizens of forming a terrorist cell linked to Iran and trained in Syria intending to overthrow the regime, and for planning terrorist attacks. Opposition parties denounced the accusations and described it as propaganda. After a few weeks, tension rose again after the controversial 2010 Bahraini parliamentary election when the main opposition bloc Al Wefaq National Islamic Society with secularist National Democratic Action Society (Waad) complained that the election system was unfair and that there were aims to reduce the number of opposition chairs in the parliament, although the first party won nearly 40% of the available seats.

Hamad attended Manama Secondary School in Bahrain. He was then sent to England to study at Applegarth College, Godalming, Surrey, and later to The Leys School, Cambridge. Hamad was then sent to military training. He started with the British Army at Mons Officer Cadet School in Aldershot, Hampshire. He graduated in September 1968. In June 1972 Hamad was awarded a leadership degree at the United States Army Command and General Staff College in Fort Leavenworth, Kansas.

Hamad bin Isa al-Khalifa (Arabic : Hmd.bn.ys.bn.slmn.al khlyf@ Hamad Bin ‘Isa Bin Salman Al Halifah, born 28 January 1950)is the King of Bahrain. Since 6 March 1999, he has been the monarch in Bahrain. He was first the emir and then became the first king on 14 February 2002. He is the son Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa’s previous and first Emir. Since 1783, the country has been ruled over by the Al Khalifa Dynasty.

His father designated Hamad as the heir apparent on 27 June 1964. He was elected chairman of the Manama municipal council and the irrigation council in 1968. On 16 February 1968, he was commissioned in the Bahrain National Guard and made its commander. He remained there until 1969, when he was named commander-in-chief for the Bahrain Defence Force. Hamad was appointed the head of Bahrain’s department of defense and vice-chairman for the administrative council in 1970. He remained in both of these offices until 1971. He was the minister for state defense from 1971 to 1988.

After Hamad took power in 1999, he focused on attaining stability in a nation riddled with profound tensions after the 1990s uprising. The King succeeded in improving the living standards and in making Bahrain a financial hub. During the period 2003–2010 the Shi’ite community accused his government of corruption, discrimination in housing and jobs, recruiting foreigners to the military services and bringing Sunni tribes from Asia to change the demographic composition of the nation.
