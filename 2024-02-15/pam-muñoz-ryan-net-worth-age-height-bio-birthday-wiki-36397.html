Pam Muñoz Ryan Biography

Pam Muñoz Ryan is one of the most popular and richest Novelist who was born on December 25, 1951 in Bakersfield, California, United States. The Mexican-American writer of such bestsellers such as Becoming Naomi Leon as well as Esperanza Rising, she received the Willa Cather Literary Award in 1999. Her nonfiction for children, When Marian Sang, was awarded two awards: the Orbis Pictus Award and the Robert F. Sibert Informational Book Award.

The book she wrote, Amelia and Eleanor Go for a Ride was an inspiration for two of Ryan’s favourite historical characters: Eleanor Roosevelt and Amelia Earhart.

The daughter of an Mexican immigrants, she pays tribute to the significance of her Hispanic heritage in a variety of her writings. Together with her spouse, she has four children.
