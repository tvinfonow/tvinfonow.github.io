Huck Seed Biography

Huck Seed is one of the most popular and richest Poker Player who was born on January 15, 1969 in California, United States. Huckleberry ”Huck” Seed, born January 15, 1969, is an American professional poker player. He is best known for his win at the Main Event of 1996 World Series of Poker.

Seed was 73rd among 6,358 participants in the 2007 Main Event.

In 2010, Seed won the World Series of Poker Tournament of Champions freeroll for $500,000.

As of October 2011, his total live tournament winnings exceed $5,900,000. He has four WSOP bracelets and 40 total cashes at the WSOP, accounting for $3,331,986 of his live tournament winnings.

Seed won his fourth career bracelet at the 2003 World Series of Poker in a $3,000 Limit Razz event. Seed defeated Phil Ivey in heads up play to win this bracelet.

Seed was born in Santa Clara in California. He grew up in Corvallis in Montana and attended Corvallis High school. Seed was a member the 1987 Montana All-State basketball squad. Seed was an electrical engineer student at California Institute of Technology. He was also a Fleming House member. Seed took a break in 1989 and began playing poker. He never returned to college. Seed was a great player for Caltech’s basketball team. He is featured in Quantum Hoops (2006).

Seed won the 1996 World Series of Poker main tournament, earning him his second bracelet as well as the $1,000,000 first-place prize. Seed reached the final table at the 1999 WSOP main tournament, but was defeated by Noel Furlong, eventual champion.
