Hideki Matsuyama Biography

Hideki Matsuyama is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on February 25, 1992 in Matsuyama, Ehime Prefecture, Japan. Hideki Matsuyama, also known as Song Shan Ying Shu, Matsuyama Hideki was born 25 February 1992 and is a Japanese professional golfer. He was the Asian Amateur Championship winner in 2010 and 2011. He is a five time PGA Tour winner and a eight-time Japan Golf Tour champion. Matsuyama was ranked the No. 2 player in the world on 19 June 2017. After his finish as runner-up at the 2017 U.S. Open, Matsuyama was ranked #2 on the Official World Golf Ranking.

Matsuyama became a professional in April 2013. He won his second professional tournament, The 2013 TsuruyaOpen on the Japan Golf Tour. Matsuyama won the Diamond Cup Golf tournament five weeks later. Matsuyama was a top-10 finisher at the 2013 U.S. Open and was promoted to the Official World Golf Ranking’s top 50. Matsuyama won his fourth Japan Golf Tour event at the Fujisankei Classic in September. Matsuyama would also win his fifth Japan Golf Tour event at the Casio Open in December. Matsuyama became the first rookie to be named as the Japan Tour’s Money List Leader.

Matsuyama earned his first PGA Tour win at the 2014 Memorial Tournament, beating Kevin Na in a playoff and moving to a career-high OWGR ranking of 13th. The win was the first for a Japanese player since Ryuji Imada in 2008. In his first full season as a PGA tour member, he finished 28th in the FedEx Cup standings.

For 2014, Matsuyama qualified for the PGA Tour through non-member earnings. In just seven PGA Tour-sanctioned events, Matsuyama had six top-25 finishes, including a T-6 at the 2013 Open Championship.

Matsuyama ranked number one in the World Amateur Golf Rankings in August 2012.

On 30 October 2016, Matsuyama followed up his Japan Open triumph by winning the WGC-HSBC Champions, colloquially known as “Asia’s Major”, in Shanghai. Matsuyama became the first Asian golfer to claim a World Golf Championship since the series was inaugurated in 1999. With the victory, Matsuyama rose to number 6 in the Official World Golf Ranking, his highest position and the second highest ever by a Japanese player after Masashi Ozaki, who achieved a ranking of fifth.
