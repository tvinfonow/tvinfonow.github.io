Top Facts about Wendell Willkie

  • Wendell Willkie was a lawyer and corporate executive.
  • He ran for president in 1940 as a Republican.
  • Willkie was the first presidential candidate to fly in an airplane during his campaign.
  • He lost the election to Franklin D. Roosevelt.
  • Willkie supported civil rights and opposed isolationism.
  • He wrote a bestselling book, “One World,” advocating for international cooperation.
  • Willkie died of a heart attack in 1944 at age 52.
  • His campaign helped shift the Republican Party towards internationalism.
  • Willkie’s law firm merged with another to become what is now WilmerHale.
  • He was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1983 by Ronald Reagan.
