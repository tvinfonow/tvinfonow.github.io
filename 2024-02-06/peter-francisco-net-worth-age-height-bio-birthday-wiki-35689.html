Peter Francisco Biography

Peter Francisco is one of the most popular and richest Snooker Player who was born on February 13, 1962 in Stellenbosch, South Africa. Peter Francisco (born 14 February 1962 in Cape Town, Western Cape) is a former South African professional snooker player who took home the African Snooker Championship 4 times and the South African Snooker Championship 8 times. He also won the South African Billiards Championship 13 times as an amateur as well as a professional.

Francisco took part in the Six-red World Championship in 2015 with five matches played within his team. He was defeated 1-5 by Marco Fu and Jamie Clarke 3-3 against Mark Williams and 4-5 to the eventual champion Thepchaiya Un-Nooh. He was eliminated following the group stage. However, he was able to beat Darren Paris 5-1 to record his first win in a competitive match following a win of 10-8 over Mick Price in the 1995 World Championship.

In June 2013 he won the ABSF African Snooker Championship.
